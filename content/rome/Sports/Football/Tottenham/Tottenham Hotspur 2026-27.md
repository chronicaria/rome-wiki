## Why it matters

The durable question is whether Tottenham can turn recruitment volume and additional training time into a coherent team. De Zerbi's earlier sides offer a prior: deep possession intended to provoke pressure, close support around the first line, rapid exploitation after a press is broken, and aggressive attempts to recover the ball. They do not prove how Spurs will play. Competitive possessions must establish which players receive under pressure, how the team protects attacks, where chances come from, and whether the coach adapts when opponents refuse to press.

Squad size creates a parallel test. Tottenham have accumulated central defenders, goalkeepers, and midfielders with plausible technical uses, but depth is valuable only when roles are complementary and the group can be registered. The last official Premier League list is a February 2026 baseline rather than the final 2026–27 submission. Under-21 exemptions and home-grown classifications must be confirmed from the next league publication rather than guessed from nationality or a website squad page.

## Working tests

1. **Build-up:** can Tottenham repeatedly create a free receiver by attracting pressure without turning every deep possession into unnecessary risk?
2. **Rest defence:** when attacks break down, are the distances and player positions behind the ball sufficient to delay or stop transitions?
3. **Role hierarchy:** do the many defenders, midfielders, goalkeepers, and forwards acquire distinct responsibilities, or does nominal depth become redundancy?
4. **Registration:** which players occupy the scarce non-home-grown senior places after the window, and which young players remain exempt?
5. **Pathway:** in a season without European group matches, which prospects have a credible route to competitive minutes and which need loans?
6. **Calendar:** does the absence of Europe produce visible tactical development, and how does domestic-cup progress alter that advantage?

These are hypotheses, not preseason conclusions. The tactical tests should normally be reviewed over four to six competitive matches with opponent quality, score state, rest, and lineup continuity recorded. Transfer announcements can change roster state immediately; player-role claims require match evidence.
