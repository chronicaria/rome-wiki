## Reading the ledger

This is an **official-completion-only state ledger** for Tottenham Hotspur's senior men's squad in the 2026/27 cycle. An entry appears only when Tottenham published a primary announcement. It does not infer a transfer from journalism, social posts, squad photographs, or another club's announcement. Undisclosed fees remain undisclosed.

The state labels deliberately separate several things that transfer coverage often collapses:

- **announced** means Tottenham publicly confirmed a signing, agreement, or move;
- **effective** means the stated joining or departure date has passed;
- **conditional** means Tottenham itself named an unresolved condition, such as a work permit, international clearance, or performance criteria;
- **permanent** describes the registration path, not whether every administrative condition has already cleared;
- **loan with conditional obligation** remains a loan unless and until the stated trigger is satisfied.

The cutoff is in New York time; it corresponds to **10 July 2026, 04:01 BST**. Later announcements belong to a subsequent ledger revision.

### State notes

**Robertson** was announced on 5 June with a future effective date: he would join on 1 July after his Liverpool contract expired. At the cutoff that date had passed, so the correct state is effective free-agent arrival—not merely an agreement and not a fee-bearing transfer unless later primary evidence says otherwise.

**Senesi** was similarly announced ahead of a 1 July start after his Bournemouth contract expired, but Tottenham added “subject to international clearance.” The calendar condition has matured; the administrative condition cannot be silently deleted. In the absence of a cited follow-up explicitly confirming clearance, this ledger preserves it.

**Van Hecke** is the cleanest completed purchase in the announcement set: Tottenham said he “joins” from Brighton and had signed a long-term contract. No qualifier appeared in the primary announcement. The fee was not disclosed there, so the ledger does not supply one.

**Dúbravka** was announced on 24 June to join on 1 July after his Burnley contract expired. This is an important distinction in squad analysis: it adds an experienced goalkeeper without the source establishing a transfer fee.

**Fernandes** was announced on 2 July as joining from West Ham. Tottenham used present-tense completion language and named no permit or clearance condition. The club did not disclose a fee in the announcement.

**Tonali** was announced on 6 July as a signing from Newcastle, explicitly **subject to work permit**. ## What is deliberately excluded

- Rumored targets, negotiations, bids, medicals, fee estimates, and contract-length claims not contained in Tottenham's announcement.
- Players returning from 2025/26 loans. A loan expiry changes squad availability but is not a new 2026/27 transfer unless the club announces a further move.
- Academy-only moves and Tottenham Women transactions. Those are valid club business but require their own ledgers because registration systems, squad purposes, and evidentiary completeness differ.
- Contract renewals. They change retention state, not club registration ownership.
- Coaching appointments, including [[Roberto De Zerbi]], because they are employment changes rather than player transfers.

## Analytical reading

The official state already shows a concentrated first-team rebuild rather than a rumor-driven shopping list. Four arrivals—Senesi, Van Hecke, Robertson, and Dúbravka—change the defensive and goalkeeping base; Fernandes and Tonali change midfield capacity. Three of the six arrivals came after contract expiry, while the announcements for the other three did not disclose fees. Therefore the primary record supports a statement about **roster composition**, but not a reliable net-spend calculation.

The state machine also exposes why headline counts can mislead. Tottenham had six officially announced senior arrivals by the cutoff, yet Tonali's work-permit qualifier and Senesi's international-clearance qualifier must remain attached. Conversely, Robertson and Dúbravka were announced in June but did not join until 1 July. Counting by announcement date and counting by effective registration date answer different questions.

On exits, Véliz and Devine are completed permanent departures. Drăgușin is not: his loan reduces immediate centre-back availability, but the longer-term asset state depends on undisclosed performance criteria. Any future revision should change his row only when Tottenham or another authoritative registration source confirms that the obligation has activated and the permanent move has become effective.

## Why it matters

A transfer ledger is useful only if it preserves what is known at each state boundary. Tottenham's summer announcements mix present-tense signings, future effective dates, expiring contracts, administrative conditions, exercised options, and a conditional purchase obligation. Treating all of them as the same kind of “done deal” obscures both current availability and future squad control.

This version supplies a reproducible baseline for later work on [[Tottenham 2026-27 squad architecture]], registration, tactical roles, and financial interpretation. It also creates a clean audit rule: add or transition an entry only when a primary source changes the official state.
