## Why it matters

The three-day sequence shows why individual transaction headlines are insufficient. Myers was accurately described as recalled on July 8 but no longer belonged on an active-roster snapshot after his July 9 option. Curry and Hammer were on the active roster after their contracts were selected, while the Carrillo and Zuñiga DFA cases had reached official resolutions. Seelinger's and Oliva's outcomes remained unresolved in the July 9 transaction log.

For the rules behind these state changes, see [[Injured-list moves and Mets roster mechanics]]. For performance claims arising from a changed role or workload, use the controls in [[How to read Mets Statcast changes without overfitting]].

This ledger records only the league's stated actions. Same-day selections and removals make the roster arithmetic legible, but they do not prove that the club formally paired one named addition with one named subtraction unless the official record says so. Warren's disclosed forearm strain and the two rehab assignments likewise do not support independent recovery forecasts.
