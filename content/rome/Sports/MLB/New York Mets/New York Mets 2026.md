## Current state

MLB's standings page, last updated July 9 at 9:35 p.m. ET, listed New York at **40–54 (.426)**: fifth in the NL East, 15 games behind Atlanta and 12 games out of the final NL Wild Card. The Mets had a minus-56 run differential and a 41–53 expected record on MLB's expanded standings. Their next scheduled game was July 10 at home against Boston.

The [[New York Mets 2026 roster and transaction ledger]] reconstructs the linked July 7–9 sequence. On July 8, New York selected Xzavion Curry's contract and recalled Tobias Myers while placing Austin Warren on the 15-day injured list with a disclosed right forearm strain and designating Matt Seelinger for assignment. On July 9, the club optioned Myers, selected Dan Hammer, designated Jared Oliva for assignment, released Alex Carrillo, and outrighted Guillo Zuñiga to Syracuse. The official active roster listed Curry and Hammer—not Myers—after those moves.

Dedniel Núñez's rehabilitation assignment moved to Syracuse and Luis Robert Jr.'s to Binghamton on July 8. Those transactions establish where the assignments continued, but they do not establish readiness, workload limits, or activation dates.
