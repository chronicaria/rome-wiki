## How to read this ledger

This is a dated control surface, not a projection. The **official label** column transcribes the Mets' 40-man page. “MLB” below means that page showed no `Minors` or injured-list marker; it should not be treated as an independently reconciled 26-man-roster audit. “Minors” proves that the player appeared as a minor leaguer on the official 40-man tab, but the page alone does not say when or how he reached that assignment.

The **counted place** column applies MLB's own note that players on the 60-day injured list appear on the 40-man tab but are not officially part of the 40-man roster. Players on the 10- or 15-day injured list remain counted. On that basis the displayed list reconciles to 40 counted places and no apparent opening. That count is a snapshot and can change with any transaction.

The **option evidence** column is deliberately narrow. A dated “optioned” entry appears only where the official transaction log supplies that verb. `Unknown` means this research did not locate an authoritative player-level statement of option years remaining; it does **not** mean that the player has no option. Likewise, this ledger does not derive Rule 5 eligibility from age, signing date, draft year, or minor-league tenure.

## What the option evidence does and does not prove

MLB says players typically receive three option years, with a fourth available in defined cases. An option year is expended only after at least 20 days in the minors on optional assignment during the season, and only one option year is used in a season even if a player is optioned and recalled multiple times. MLB also limits a player to five optional assignments in one season, after which another minor-league assignment requires outright waivers.

Those general rules cannot safely be turned into player-level answers from this snapshot alone. For Mauricio and Myers, the transaction record establishes at least one 2026 optional assignment. It does not establish whether either has crossed the 20-day threshold, how many times either has been optioned this season, whether an exception affects a minimum stay, or how many option years will remain after 2026. For the other ten players labeled `Minors`, the roster page does not itself distinguish an optional assignment from every other possible procedural history. A transaction-history audit is required before upgrading those rows.

Service-time and consent rights are also outside this ledger. No player's ability to reject an assignment is inferred from age, career length, contract reputation, or memory. Those are player-specific legal facts requiring authoritative service and transaction evidence.

## Rule 5 boundary

MLB's general rule says a player signed at age 18 or younger must be added to the 40-man roster within five seasons to avoid Rule 5 eligibility, while a player signed at 19 or older must be protected within four seasons. A player on the counted 40-man roster is protected from selection in the annual major-league phase of the Rule 5 Draft.

Therefore, the 40 counted players above are affirmatively documented as current 40-man members and protected by that status at this snapshot. This ledger makes **no player-level Rule 5 claim** about Mets minor leaguers outside the displayed list. Correctly evaluating those players requires, at minimum, authoritative identity, age-at-signing, first-contract date, seasons elapsed, prior reserve-list treatment, and any special eligibility facts. Draft year by itself is not enough, and the official 40-man page is not a complete list of Rule 5-eligible players.

The six IL-60 players are separated rather than labeled “Rule 5 protected” here because MLB expressly says they are not officially part of the 40-man count while listing them on the tab. Their reserve-list treatment is not independently documented in the sources reviewed for this ledger, so a categorical player-level label would outrun the evidence.

## Why it matters

The ledger identifies the Mets' immediate flexibility without pretending to know private plans. Twelve counted players sit in the minors, giving the club a group that can potentially be recalled without selecting a new contract, subject to active-roster room and each player's procedural eligibility. The full counted roster means a non-roster selection, waiver claim, trade acquisition that adds a net player, or 60-day-IL activation would also require a 40-man solution unless an intervening move creates space.

The most important missing variable is not a speculative depth-chart ranking but verified option control. A player can be on the 40-man roster and in the minors while still presenting very different future constraints depending on option years, assignment count, service rights, and waiver history. Until those data are sourced, `Minors` should be read as a current location label—not as a claim of unlimited shuttle flexibility.

## Methodology and verification

1. Read the Mets' official 40-man page on July 10, 2026 and transcribed every displayed player and status marker.
2. Applied MLB's explicit page note that IL-60 players appear on the tab but are not officially part of the 40-man roster.
3. Counted the displayed groups and reconciled 40 counted places plus six IL-60 players.
4. Checked the official July 7 and July 9 Mets transaction pages for recent player-level option verbs. Mauricio and Myers are the only optional assignments asserted here.
5. Used MLB's transaction glossary for the general mechanics of options, 40-man protection, and Rule 5 timing.
6. Withheld all unverified option-year counts, assignment counts, service-time rights, contract rights, medical projections, and non-roster Rule 5 eligibility judgments.

Verification is **primary-source, snapshot-complete for displayed membership and labels** and **partial for player-level option history**. It is intentionally incomplete for players outside the 40-man display and for option years remaining.
