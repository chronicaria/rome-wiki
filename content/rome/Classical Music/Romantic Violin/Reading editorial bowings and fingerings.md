Violin bowings and fingerings become historical evidence only when composer, first print, dedicatee, teacher, and later editor layers are attributed separately.

Up: [[Romantic violin repertoire]]

## What a mark can mean

A slur may define grouping, articulation, or literal bowing, and its force depends on genre, date, source, and surrounding notation. Fingering can solve execution, shape color through string choice, expose a shift as rhetoric, or transmit a school tradition. Modern performer editions often make these choices explicit but may visually mingle them with composer text.

Read full score and solo part together. First identify the edition and whether additions are typographically distinguished; compare autograph or composer-supervised print where available; then attribute dedicatee or editor markings. A persuasive performance-practice claim explains the musical consequence at a passage and retains alternatives. An unattributed fingering cannot establish what the composer intended or what a national school did.
