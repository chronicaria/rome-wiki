The French, German, and English first editions of Chopin's Op. 28 form a transmission network, so an apparent difference must be traced before it becomes a performance instruction.

Up: [[Romantic piano repertoire]]

## A bounded edition laboratory

Op. 28 appeared through different national publishers, with distinct dedications in the French/English and German issues. The English proofs were engraved from the French edition, while the wider source history includes manuscript and print relationships that cannot be reduced to publication date. The Online Chopin Variorum Edition identifies witnesses as FFE, GFE, and EFE and permits bar-level comparison.

The responsible laboratory starts with one numbered prelude and one parameter—pitch, rhythm, articulation, pedal, or layout. Record the exact copy and impression; compare images; identify shared errors or corrections; then consult a critical commentary. Until that work is done, a divergence is a variant, not proof that Chopin authorized two expressive solutions.
