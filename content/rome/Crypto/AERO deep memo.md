AERO has the strongest current fee engine in the top three, but value capture requires veAERO locking and the July 2026 Aero migration is a real execution gate.



## Current Read

July 6, 2026 snapshot: price about $0.58-$0.59, market cap about $561M-$564M, current-supply FDV about $1.12B-$1.13B, circulating supply about 964M AERO, total supply about 1.93B, and infinite max supply because emissions continue.

Aerodrome showed about $331.8M TVL, all on Base; $13.8B 30d DEX volume; $7.94M 30d fees; $4.45M 30d holder revenue to veAERO voters; $7.05M 30d incentives; and about -$2.6M 30d earnings. Base DEX share was roughly 51%-52%.

## What Works

Aerodrome is real Base infrastructure. It is the main Base liquidity hub, has visible fees, high lock participation, and better token liquidity than most smaller DeFi names.

The veAERO model routes fees and voting incentives to active lockers. That is a real value-capture mechanism if the investor is willing to lock, vote well, delegate to Relay, and accept strategy and duration risk.

## What The Deep Pass Changed

Liquid AERO is not the fee-capture asset. It is a dilutable option to become a veAERO operator. Passive liquid holders absorb emissions and only benefit indirectly if demand to lock AERO rises.

Emissions are still the core issue. On-chain reads put tail emissions around 21 bps/week, roughly 10.9% simple annualized to gauges, and roughly 12.7% simple annualized after rebase/team emissions. Current 30d incentives exceed holder revenue.

The Aero migration is not cosmetic. It combines Aerodrome and Velodrome under one token/governance/economic framework, changes NFTs and frontends, retires gauges/factories, and may alter voter participation mechanics. That is a near-term diligence gate.

## Main Risks

- Base concentration: Aerodrome is effectively a Base beta trade.
- Emissions reflexivity: if AERO price falls, incentives buy less liquidity, which can reduce volume and fee capture.
- Migration risk: users, LPs, veNFT holders, routers, and CEXs must transition cleanly.
- Governance concentration: emergency council, factory, gauge, fee, managed veNFT, and Momentum Fund controls matter.
- Front-end risk: Aerodrome had a prior DNS/front-end compromise with user losses, even though contracts were not the failure point.
- Competition: Uniswap and PancakeSwap remain meaningful Base competitors.

## Promotion Gates

Promote if most are true for two consecutive months:

- 30d DEX volume above $18B-$20B.
- Base DEX share above 55%.
- 30d fees above $10M and holder revenue above $6M.
- Holder revenue exceeds incentives, or earnings are at least break-even.
- Locked/deposited AERO stays above 52%-55% of supply.
- Tail emissions stay at or below 21 bps/week.
- Aero migration launches with audits, CEX/router support, high migration rate, and no fee-capture haircut.

## Kill Conditions

- 30d volume below $8B for two months.
- Base DEX share below 35%-40%.
- Holder revenue below $2.5M while incentives remain above $6M.
- Incentives exceed 2x holder revenue for two months.
- Tail emissions rise above 30 bps/week or total mint inflation trends above 18% annualized.
- Lock ratio falls below 45%.
- Migration weakens veAERO economics, introduces unclear terms, or causes major liquidity fragmentation.
- Another front-end/contract incident causes more than $1M of user losses.
