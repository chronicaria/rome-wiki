SYRUP is the most venture-like top-three candidate: Maple is real credit infrastructure, but the token depends on underwriting, liquidity, governance, and discretionary buyback policy.



## Current Read

July 6, 2026 snapshot: price about $0.173-$0.174, market cap around $195M-$207M, FDV around $209M-$216M, and 24h volume around $17M-$19M depending on source.

Maple's transparency page showed about $4.51B AUM, $20.73M LTM protocol revenue, and $1.22M monthly protocol revenue. DefiLlama showed about $2.36B TVL, $1.90B active loans, $7.58M 30d gross fees, $914K 30d protocol revenue, and about $229K 30d holder revenue / buyback allocation.

## What Works

Maple is not vapor. It has real lender supply, active borrower demand, structured-credit integrations, stablecoin-yield distribution, and serious partner/channel surface area. The Kraken warehouse facility and Robinhood/USDG-style distribution are qualitatively better evidence than most crypto partnerships.

The valuation is not obviously absurd if revenue compounds. A roughly $200M token against $20M-ish LTM protocol revenue is interesting, provided the token keeps a durable claim on buybacks and Maple avoids credit losses.

## What The Deep Pass Changed

The biggest risk is credit, not smart contracts. Maple's own docs describe defaults, impairments, withdrawal queues, collateral, oracle, legal, and admin risks. The 2022 Orthogonal default remains relevant because it showed how off-chain borrower misrepresentation can break onchain credit.

Buybacks are not a hard property right. MIP-019 moved 25% of protocol revenue into the Syrup Strategic Fund for buybacks, cash allocation, and balance-sheet building; MIP-020 extended the framework through H1 2026. The agents did not find a passed H2 2026 continuation.

Borrower concentration is high. One agent's public GraphQL pull found 69 active open-term loans, 29 borrower addresses, top-5 borrowers around 56% of active principal, and top-10 around 85%. That makes underwriting transparency central.

## Main Risks

- Credit/default loss: one impairment can reduce lender NAV, AUM, revenue, and reputation.
- Withdrawal queues: FIFO liquidity can become delayed or haircut during stress.
- Borrower opacity: KYC and underwriting are better than anonymous DeFi, but not the same as public borrower solvency.
- Buyback discretion: SSF policy can be paused, redirected, or changed by governance.
- Legal access: syrupUSDC/USDT products are not available to U.S. persons.
- Supply/migration trust: MPL-to-SYRUP conversion closure and supply-document discrepancies are governance scars.

## Promotion Gates

Promote only if most are true:

- H2 2026 SSF/buyback allocation is formally renewed at 25% or better.
- Monthly protocol revenue exceeds $2M for several months.
- Active loans stay above $2.5B and official AUM remains above $4.5B-$5B.
- Top-5 borrower concentration falls below about 40%, or Maple publishes enough borrower/collateral disclosure to justify the concentration.
- No material impairment/default causes lender NAV loss or withdrawal penalties.
- Supply reconciles across docs, onchain total, circulating supply, SSF, treasury, migrator, and remaining emissions.
- DEX liquidity rises above $5M and live CEX depth supports intended sizing.

## Kill Conditions

- H2 2026 buybacks are paused, cut below 15%, or absorbed into vague balance-sheet uses.
- Any lender loss/default exceeds 0.5%-1.0% of AUM.
- Active loans fall below $1B while AUM remains high.
- Monthly protocol revenue falls below $500K for two consecutive months.
- SSF SYRUP is sold into the market for operating expenses.
- Collateral mix shifts materially into long-tail/high-beta assets.
- Official AUM, DefiLlama TVL, and active-loan data diverge by more than 20% without reconciliation.
