AERO has strong Base DEX product-market fit and real veAERO fee capture, but emissions and Base concentration must be treated as core risks.

Up: [[Crypto]]


## Snapshot

July 6, 2026 snapshot: about $0.59 price, $564M market cap, $1.13B FDV, $33M 24h token volume, and $119M token liquidity.

Score: 71/100. Upside: 7/10. Kill risk: 5/10.

## Evidence

Aerodrome showed $332.8M TVL, all on Base; $13.8B 30d DEX volume; $7.94M 30d fees; $4.45M 30d holder revenue; $7.05M 30d incentives; and negative 30d earnings around -$2.6M. It is one of the clearest usage names in this crypto set, but incentives still exceed holder revenue.

Value capture only works through veAERO. veAERO voters direct emissions and receive fees plus external incentives from the pools they vote for. Liquid AERO is an option to lock, not a direct fee claim.

Supply risk is ongoing emissions, not a one-time unlock. Tail emissions around 21 bps/week imply roughly low-double-digit annualized dilution before rebases/team emissions. Base concentration is both moat and fragility.

## Bull case

Base keeps growing as a major consumer chain, Aerodrome remains its liquidity hub, fee growth outruns emissions, and AERO expansion creates a multi-chain liquidity layer.

## Bear case

Fee capture is emissions-reflexive. If bribes fade, Base share leaks, or another front-end/security event hurts trust, veAERO yield compresses and liquid AERO can reprice hard.

## Gates

Promote if two consecutive months show 30d DEX volume above $20B, 30d fees above $10M, holder revenue above $6M, earnings at or above $0, Base DEX share at least 50% or non-Base Aero volume at least 20%, and locked AERO at least 50% of supply.

Demote if 30d DEX volume falls below $8B for two months, Base DEX share falls below 35%, holder revenue falls below $2.5M while incentives stay above $6M, locked AERO falls below 45%, or another exploit/front-end incident causes more than $1M user losses.
