SYRUP has real onchain credit usage and a buyback link, but Maple remains a credit-cycle bet with default, supply, and policy-risk scars.

Up: [[Crypto]]


## Snapshot

July 6, 2026 snapshot: about $0.174 price, $205M market cap, $215M FDV, $18.5M 24h volume, and roughly 1.19B circulating SYRUP.

Score: 71/100. Upside: 6.5/10. Kill risk: 7/10.

## Evidence

Maple has visible usage: about $2.36B TVL, $1.90B active loans, $7.58M 30d borrower fees/interest, about $914K 30d retained revenue, and about $229K 30d holder-revenue/buyback allocation. Maple's own site cites much larger lifetime borrowing and loan counts.

Token capture is real but not final. Maple states that 25% of protocol revenue funds SYRUP buybacks, but the H2 2026 continuation needs confirmation. The prior MPL-to-SYRUP migration and supply reporting discrepancies create governance and trust surfaces.

Credit risk is the central issue. Maple's 2022 Orthogonal default was about $36M and exposed how institutional underwriting can fail when borrower/counterparty losses are hidden.

## Bull case

Maple becomes the credit and yield layer for stablecoin distribution across fintech, exchanges, and DeFi, driving AUM above $5B and retained revenue high enough for buybacks to matter versus a roughly $200M market cap.

## Bear case

AUM is hot-money yield chasing; crypto collateral and borrower quality remain correlated; a market shock creates impairments or withdrawal queues; and governance reduces or delays buybacks.

## Gates

Promote if active loans stay above $2.3B, TVL above $3.0B, 30d retained revenue above $1.5M, buybacks above $375K 30d, H2 2026 buyback allocation is formally extended at at least 25%, and supply reporting reconciles within 2%.

Demote if active loans fall below $1.25B for 30 days, retained revenue stays below $500K 30d for two months, lender losses exceed 0.25% TVL or 1% active loans, secured-pool collateral ratio falls below 120%, or governance cuts buybacks below 15%.
