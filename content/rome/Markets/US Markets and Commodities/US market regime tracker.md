The July 2026 macro setup combines slower payroll growth and downward revisions with elevated headline inflation and an EIA forecast of lower oil prices after Hormuz reopening assumptions.

Up: [[Markets]]

## Data state

The June employment report, published July 2, showed payrolls up 57,000, unemployment at 4.2%, participation at 61.5%, and average hourly earnings up 3.5% year over year; April and May payrolls were revised down by 74,000 combined.

May CPI, published June 10, rose 0.5% month over month and 4.2% year over year, while core CPI was 2.9% year over year. June CPI is scheduled for July 14.

EIA's July outlook, published July 7, incorporated a June 18 Hormuz memorandum and forecast Brent around $74 per barrel in the third quarter and $65 in 2027. These are forecasts, not observed prices.

## Cross-asset confirmation panel

The latest aligned daily observations available during this scan were mostly July 8. The 2-year Treasury yield was 4.21%, the 10-year nominal yield 4.56%, the 10-year real yield 2.31%, and the 10-year breakeven 2.25%. Relative to July 6, nominal yields rose 8 basis points at both maturities, while the real yield rose 7 basis points and the breakeven only 1 basis point. That decomposition fits a real-rate or term-premium move better than a fresh inflation-expectations shock, though two days are not a regime.

High-yield option-adjusted spreads were 2.70% on July 8, versus 2.72% July 6, and VIX closed at 16.90 versus 15.57. Credit did not confirm acute risk-off stress even as equity volatility firmed. The Fed's broad dollar index was 120.69 on July 2, below 121.15 on July 1; its slower publication cadence prevents a same-day causal comparison.

Commodity data were asynchronous. FRED's EIA-sourced Brent spot series was $69.56 on July 6 and Henry Hub natural gas was $3.29 per million Btu. EIA's outlook is conditional on restored Hormuz flows and projected inventory rebuilding, not a live curve. USDA's July WASDE is scheduled for July 10 at noon Eastern, so no July agricultural-balance claim is yet admissible.

## Why it matters

The next step is cross-asset confirmation across nominal and real yields, breakevens, equities and breadth, the dollar, credit, volatility, and commodity curves. Causality should not be assigned until event timing and confounders are checked.
