A political-market backtest needs a prospective missing-data policy because absent quotes, vanished contracts, voids, and ambiguous outcomes are different failures, often caused by the same liquidity and uncertainty the study is trying to measure.

## Missing is not one state

Suppose a study asks for the probability of each eligible political event 30 days before its scheduled date. At one cutoff the collector times out. At another it retrieves a book with an ask but no bid. A third contract has disappeared from the public listing endpoint. A fourth traded normally but was later voided. A fifth has prices and a final payout, yet its archived rules are too incomplete to establish what proposition those prices represented. All five rows can become `NA` in a score table. They do not mean the same thing.

The distinction matters because every repair embeds a claim about the missingness mechanism. Carrying forward a quote assumes the unobserved forecast is adequately represented by an older market state. Taking the next trade assumes later information did not matter. Replacing an empty bid with zero assumes nobody willing to quote is equivalent to an executable zero-cent valuation. Deleting a void assumes the forecast universe excludes questions that become operationally difficult. Treating a vanished market as never listed assumes the venue's later catalog preserves its historical opportunity set. None of those claims is automatic.

The first rule is therefore architectural: keep a **coverage ledger** containing every event generated by the frozen universe rule, then derive score tables for explicitly named estimands. Do not let a database join decide the sample. A left join from the frozen event-horizon manifest to captured observations preserves absences; an inner join silently converts capture and venue failures into exclusions.

## A stage-based taxonomy

Each eligible event-horizon row should receive one primary status at the earliest stage where it fails, plus secondary flags that preserve later facts. A useful decision order is:

1. **Universe and listing:** Was the event eligible, and was a matching contract listed under the prospective rule?
2. **Lifecycle:** Had the contract opened by the cutoff, and was it active, halted, delisted, closed, or replaced?
3. **Capture:** Did the research system obtain the required point-in-time artifact?
4. **Quote admissibility:** Did the artifact contain fresh, two-sided, sufficiently deep executable quotes under the registered size and fee rule?
5. **Semantic admissibility:** Can the archived contract version be mapped to the target proposition without unresolved meaning changes?
6. **Outcome admissibility:** Did the contract and substantive event reach the terminal state required by the estimand?

This order prevents double counting. A collector can fail on a contract that later voids; `raw_capture_failed` can be the primary forecast-status code while `void` remains a terminal-outcome flag. The complete record should preserve both.

### Capture failure

`raw_capture_failed` means the observation should have been obtainable under the design, but the research pipeline did not preserve it. Causes include network failure, rate limiting, authentication or access changes, parser breakage, clock skew, corrupt storage, or a missed scheduler run. This is primarily a measurement-system failure, not evidence that the venue lacked a forecast.

Record the request time, target endpoint and parameters, collector and parser versions, error class, response status when available, retry count, last successful observation, next successful observation, and whether an independent official endpoint was checked. Retries may occur only within the prospectively defined observation window. A successful request after the cutoff is provenance about recovery, not the forecast that existed at the cutoff.

Capture failure can be plausibly unrelated to the unobserved price when caused by a random local outage, but it can also be informative. Traffic spikes around debates or surprise withdrawals may overload both the collector and venue precisely when prices move most. API schema changes can affect particular old or exceptional contracts. Rate limits may preferentially drop high-frequency markets. The coverage report should therefore tabulate failures by venue, time, political event type, observed volatility before failure, and lifecycle status rather than assuming a benign mechanism.

### One-sided or non-executable books

An observed but one-sided book is not a missing raw record. It is an observed market state that may lack the point forecast required by the registered extractor. If only a Yes ask is present, the analyst knows an offered purchase price at a stated quantity, not the best executable sale price. Coding the missing bid as zero manufactures the interval $[0,a]$; coding it as the complement of the same side can double-count a quote unless the venue's contract and order-book representation actually justify that transformation. [[Order-book microstructure of event contracts]] explains why Yes and No complementarity depends on payout and venue mechanics.

Preserve each side, price level, quantity, exchange timestamp, quote age, tick size, status, and any venue-native empty-book indicator. Use `one_sided_book`, `empty_book`, `insufficient_depth`, `stale_bid`, or `stale_ask` as analysis-relevant flags. A primary midpoint estimand should classify the point forecast as unavailable when either admissible side is absent. A distinct interval or partial-identification analysis may use valid observed bounds, but it must state what economic assumptions produce the unobserved bound.

One-sidedness is often informative: market makers may withdraw under adverse-selection risk, contract ambiguity, inventory imbalance, technical stress, or imminent resolution. Deleting these rows can make remaining forecasts look more liquid, stable, and calibrated than the venue was as a usable forecasting instrument.

### Delisting, suspension, and replacement

A delisted market must remain in the event manifest. Distinguish `delisted_before_cutoff`, `suspended_at_cutoff`, `closed_early`, `replaced_by_new_contract`, and `catalog_record_missing`. A current API that omits a contract does not establish that it never existed. Preserve initial listing artifacts, immutable venue identifiers, lifecycle-event history, the last observed rules, and the relationship to any replacement.

Kalshi's official lifecycle documentation distinguishes active trading, close, determination, amendment, and settlement-related fields. Those states should not be collapsed into “available” versus “missing.” A market can be closed to new trading yet still await determination; an amendment can alter timing without erasing its prior history. Likewise, a relisted or replacement contract should not be spliced onto the original merely because its title is similar. Apply [[Versioning political event-contract semantics]] and record whether the replacement is semantically equivalent, economically continuous, both, or neither.

Delisting is especially likely to be outcome- or difficulty-related. Venues may remove markets because of legal risk, ambiguous rules, event cancellation, low interest, or operational problems. Complete-case analysis then selects for contracts that remained easy to administer. Report delisting as an operational outcome even when no binary score is possible.

### Voids, disputes, and nonbinary terminal states

A void is observed terminal information, not an unknown binary outcome. A pending dispute is a known procedural state with an unknown final payout. A split payout or refund is an economic outcome that may not correspond to $y\in\{0,1\}$. Use separate values such as `yes_final`, `no_final`, `void_refund`, `split_payout`, `disputed_pending`, `revised_after_initial`, `postponed_open`, and `unresolved_at_freeze`. Preserve the venue settlement vector, the real-world fact under the named authoritative source, and the researcher's semantic mapping as distinct fields.

Polymarket's documented resolution process includes proposal, a challenge period, repeated proposal or dispute, and potential escalation to UMA's Data Verification Mechanism. A proposal is therefore not a final outcome. Kalshi also documents determination and settlement as lifecycle stages rather than a single undifferentiated end timestamp. The backtest must use the state available at its declared analysis freeze and later append revisions; it must not overwrite a disputed history with the eventual final label.

Ordinary binary Brier or log loss is undefined for a void unless the study prospectively defines a different target. Coding a refund as $y=0.5$ pretends the political event occurred halfway and changes the scoring problem. Coding every void as a loss measures an operational penalty but is no longer a proper score for the binary event. Both may be legitimate in a separately named economic-utility estimand; neither belongs silently in a binary forecast-accuracy table.

### Semantic uncertainty

`semantic_version_uncertain` applies when prices were captured but the proposition cannot be reconstructed well enough to score. Missing rule text, an undated clarification, conflicting resolution sources, an unclear amendment boundary, or a questionable cross-venue match can create this state. It is not cured by the existence of a payout. The venue may have paid Yes while the researcher remains unable to show that the archived forecast question matched the target defined by the study.

Store the rule artifacts available at the cutoff, content hashes, clarification times, named sources and fallbacks, reviewer judgment, alternative plausible interpretations, and a confidence or adjudication status. Do not allow a present-day page to retroactively supply historical semantics. When two interpretations would generate different labels or inclusion decisions, exclude the row from the confirmatory semantic estimand and show both mappings in sensitivity analysis if evidence supports them.

Semantic uncertainty is predictably concentrated in politically unusual cases—withdrawals, vacancies, delayed certifications, procedural votes, court intervention, and disputed authority. Removing it can produce an evaluation of easy-to-word politics rather than political forecasting generally.

## Missing-data mechanisms are diagnoses, not labels

Rubin's framework distinguishes missingness that is independent of observed and unobserved data, missingness explainable by observed data, and missingness that still depends on the unseen value after conditioning. The familiar shorthand is MCAR, MAR, and MNAR. These are assumptions about a probability distribution, not synonyms for administrative codes.

Let $R_i=1$ when the forecast needed for event-horizon row $i$ is admissibly observed, $P_i$ be its latent or unavailable forecast, $Y_i$ its outcome, and $X_i$ recorded covariates. A missing-at-random claim has the form

$$
\Pr(R_i=1\mid P_i,Y_i,X_i)=\Pr(R_i=1\mid Y_i,X_i).
$$

For rows with a final observed binary outcome, it says that after conditioning on that outcome and the chosen observed covariates, observability no longer depends on the missing forecast. It is not validated merely because the dataset contains many covariates. If the outcome is also missing, MAR must instead be stated relative to whatever components of that row were actually observed; the displayed formula no longer applies unchanged. In prediction markets, a more realistic mechanism often links $R_i$ to latent liquidity, volatility, ambiguity, surprise, trader attention, or venue intervention—variables also related to forecast extremity and error.

Examples clarify the limits. A power failure at the researcher's machine may approximate MCAR over a short interval, unless it occurs during a storm or news spike that also affects markets. Missing depth may be MAR conditional on contract age, category, time of day, prior volume, and spread, but only if unobserved contemporaneous volatility adds no residual effect. One-sided books and delistings are often MNAR for the point-score problem because quote withdrawal or removal can respond to information and resolution difficulty not fully captured in $X_i$.

Little's pattern-mixture perspective is useful here: compare the distribution of observed covariates and outcomes across missingness patterns rather than burying all failures in one indicator. A contract absent because it was never listed belongs to a different pattern from one captured until an ambiguous event caused suspension. Pattern tables do not identify the missing forecasts, but they reveal where complete-case claims cease to generalize.

## Choose the estimand before the remedy

There is no universal “correct” way to handle missing political-market data because different questions target different populations.

The **available-forecast accuracy estimand** asks: among eligible rows where the venue supplied an admissible forecast under the protocol, how accurate were those forecasts? Complete-case scoring can estimate this descriptive quantity. Its denominator must be visible, and the conclusion cannot be generalized to all eligible political events without additional assumptions.

The **forecast-service estimand** asks: if a user relied on the venue at every registered cutoff, what accuracy and coverage would the service deliver? This requires two outcomes: score conditional on availability and availability itself. A service with excellent Brier loss on 40% coverage is not equivalent to one with slightly worse loss on 95% coverage. A composite utility can penalize absence, but the penalty is a user decision, not a proper scoring rule; publish results across plausible penalties.

The **listed-contract estimand** asks about every contract in the frozen venue universe, including later delistings, voids, and unresolved cases. Report binary forecast performance on the eligible resolved subset plus operational failure rates on the full set. If a single number is demanded, define a decision problem with explicit costs for no quote, void, delayed settlement, and error.

The **substantive political-event estimand** asks how well markets forecast a political target independent of venue settlement. It needs a prospectively defined authoritative outcome and semantic mapping. Venue voids do not necessarily erase the real-world outcome, but a missing or mismatched forecast remains missing. This estimand must not switch between venue payout and political fact case by case.

The **matched-comparator estimand** asks which system performed better where both produced admissible forecasts. It should use identical event-horizon pairs and report paired loss differences. A separate coverage comparison is essential because matching may select the easiest shared cases. Imputing the market when only the model is present, or vice versa, changes the target and should be a registered sensitivity analysis.

## Primary policy and sensitivity analyses

A defensible primary policy is conservative:

1. Freeze the candidate universe and scheduled cutoffs before outcome inspection.
2. Never substitute a later or earlier quote for a missing cutoff in the confirmatory analysis.
3. Require the registered two-sided depth, freshness, spread, fee, and semantic rules for a point forecast.
4. Score only final binary outcomes for the binary proper-score estimand.
5. Retain every other row in the coverage ledger with a primary code, secondary flags, and lifecycle history.
6. Report the flow from eligible events to listed, captured, quote-admissible, semantically admissible, binary-final, and comparator-matched rows.
7. Compare observed characteristics and failure rates across missingness patterns, venue, horizon, event family, and election cycle.

Sensitivity analysis should then test assumptions rather than conceal them. Useful analyses include:

- **Tolerance grids:** Repeat results over prospectively bounded quote-age, spread, and depth thresholds, showing both score and coverage.
- **Interval scoring bounds:** For partially observed or wide books, compute the range of Brier or log loss over defensible probability bounds rather than inventing a midpoint.
- **Worst- and best-case bounds:** Assign missing forecasts the loss-minimizing and loss-maximizing values permitted by an explicit range. These wide bounds honestly show what the observed data alone identify.
- **Pattern-mixture shifts:** For a sensitivity parameter $\delta$, posit that missing forecasts are shifted on a chosen scale relative to predictions from observed covariates, and plot conclusions over substantively plausible $\delta$. Do not present one untestable shift as recovered truth.
- **Selection models:** Model observability jointly with forecast error only when exclusion restrictions or strong domain assumptions can be defended. Report how conclusions depend on them.
- **Void and dispute policies:** Compare binary-final-at-freeze, eventually-final, and substantive-outcome analyses without overwriting the original terminal state.
- **Leave-pattern-out results:** Show whether venue rankings survive exclusion of delistings, semantic-review cases, particular cycles, and high-volatility capture windows.
- **Service-penalty curves:** Vary the utility penalty for an unavailable forecast, keeping conditional accuracy and coverage visible beside the composite.

Multiple imputation is not a default cure. Standard imputation can propagate observed relationships under an MAR model, but it does not reconstruct an executable quote that never existed. For capture failure with rich adjacent book data, imputation may support a clearly labeled latent-price sensitivity analysis. For a one-sided market, delisting, or ambiguity-driven suspension, it risks replacing a venue failure with a model forecast and then crediting the venue for the model's answer. Imputed rows must never be described as observed market performance.

## Minimum audit fields

The row-level audit record should make every exclusion reproducible. At minimum preserve:

- stable event, contract, semantic-version, venue, and dependence-cluster identifiers;
- target proposition, full rules artifact, resolution source, fallback, and content hash;
- scheduled cutoff, venue timestamp, ingestion timestamp, timezone, and permitted observation window;
- listing, open, halt, close, delist, amendment, determination, dispute, finalization, and settlement timestamps, each labeled scheduled, observed, inferred, or missing;
- raw request URL or endpoint and parameters, response status, raw artifact hash and storage pointer, collector/parser/schema versions, retries, and error class;
- bids, asks, quantities, depth levels, last trade and time, quote ages, spread, tick size, fees, order-book status, and the exact admissibility rule result;
- primary missingness code, secondary flags, decision rule version, decision time, automated reason, reviewer, review status, and superseded-record pointer;
- venue payout state and vector, substantive outcome and authority, evidence publication time, mapping rationale, dispute and revision history;
- estimand eligibility flags for each published analysis, imputation or bound method if any, sensitivity parameters, and parent artifact or code version.

Use append-only corrections. If a late archive proves that a supposed capture failure was actually a suspended market, append a corrected classification linked to the original. Reproducibility requires knowing what the analysis believed at each freeze, not merely seeing the latest cleaned row.

## Why it matters

Missingness is where a backtest's clean numerical claim meets the institution that generated the data. Political markets are most likely to become thin, ambiguous, suspended, disputed, or operationally stressed when politics becomes unusual. Those are not peripheral cases. They are the environments in which a forecasting system's availability and contract design matter most.

A backtest that silently deletes failures can answer a narrow conditional question while sounding universal. A backtest that fills gaps with later prices introduces leakage. One that imputes venue outages from a statistical model may end up evaluating the imputation model. One that treats voids as ordinary outcomes confuses forecast accuracy with settlement utility. A good missing-data policy prevents these category errors by preserving the full opportunity set, naming the estimand, and showing how conclusions move under explicit assumptions.

The goal is not to force every contract into a score. It is to distinguish what the data identify from what an analyst assumes. Sometimes the honest conclusion is that observed forecasts performed well conditional on two-sided availability, while the evidence is insufficient to claim performance over all eligible political events. That narrower result is more useful because another researcher can reproduce it, challenge its assumptions, and design a better capture system for the next cycle.
