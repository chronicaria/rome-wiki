The CFTC's June 2026 proposal would create a contract-review framework, but it remains proposed regulation and does not settle every political contract's status.

Up: [[Political prediction markets]] · Related: [[Kalshi]] · [[Prediction-market resolution rules]]

## Proposed framework

The Commission proposes factors and procedures for deciding whether event contracts involving activities enumerated in the Commodity Exchange Act are contrary to the public interest. The document follows a March 2026 advance notice and roughly 3,500 comments, about 300 of which the CFTC characterized as detailed.

The proposal contemplates review after a market submits or self-certifies a contract, possible requests to suspend listing or trading during review, and consolidated treatment of similar contracts. The CFTC acknowledges that contracts could trade before an adverse determination and positions could consequently be closed.

Relevant proposed factors include suitable settlement criteria and dispute resolution, surveillance, customer identification, and controls on traders likely to possess nonpublic information. These are regulatory considerations, not evidence that any price is calibrated or manipulation-free.

## Why it matters

Rule uncertainty is part of contract basis risk. A political market can have clear election semantics yet still carry listing, suspension, closeout, jurisdiction, and access risks that impede cross-venue comparison.
