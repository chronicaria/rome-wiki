Rome now expands through nineteen independent subject committees that launch together every hour, followed by a dedicated half-hour publisher.

## The hourly wave

At the top of every hour, all nineteen topics begin separate research runs: the 2026 US midterms; the 2027 French presidential election; applied PDEs; exometeorology; the NBA and Dallas Mavericks; Duke men's basketball; Duke football; Tottenham; the Colts; the Mets; fantasy basketball; US markets and commodities; top-100 crypto; political prediction markets; frontier AI; Korean history; European history from 500 to 1914; Romantic piano through 1945; and Romantic violin through 1945.

Each lane owns a separate article route and persistent state. It uses the coordinator and up to three delegated workers when Codex exposes them, then refills available slots through research, long-form writing, verification, and editing. Triggering all nineteen together maximizes parallel opportunity, though the runtime may still queue or resource-manage actual execution.

## Collision-free publishing

Research lanes do not append to the same registry, run log, root MOCs, homepage ledger, or generated site files. They write topic-local work plus unique publisher receipts. At half past the hour, the twentieth committee serially integrates completed receipts, applies shared links, updates the editorial records, synchronizes the public snapshot, validates it, and publishes the new edition to GitHub Pages when every gate passes. Work that finishes after the cutoff appears in the next edition.

## Production ambition

The individual bands sum to roughly 63–145 substantial completions per ideal hourly wave, or 1,512–3,480 across twenty-four ideal waves, with no ceiling. These figures describe ambition rather than guaranteed throughput. Evidence, depth, runtime capacity, deduplication, privacy, and safety decide what is actually published.

Standard explanatory articles require 2,000–4,000 useful body words; major dossiers may require 4,000–8,000. Two-paragraph pages, padded prose, source dumps, and lightly expanded notices do not count. Ledgers, timelines, source maps, and MOC edits remain valuable but are reported separately.

## Priorities and safeguards

Dallas Mavericks/NBA leaguewide, Duke men's basketball, and Duke football retain the largest sports allocations. Tottenham keeps a higher evidence threshold. Markets emphasizes US equities, rates, credit, FX transmission, derivatives, and commodity chains. Crypto tracks a provider-ranked top 100 with identity controls. Piano and violin coverage follows Romantic and post-Romantic traditions through 1945.

Current claims carry as-of dates; official or primary evidence leads; forecasts, market prices, rumors, model outputs, and inference stay labeled. Committees do not trade, spend money, change fantasy rosters, contact people, access private accounts, or rewrite Andrew's `source: me` prose. The publisher can commit and deploy only the approved public website repository after privacy and build gates pass.

## Operating requirements

The computer must remain on, the Codex desktop app must be running, and the Rome and website projects must remain available. Codex can resource-manage concurrency even when every schedule shares the same start time.
