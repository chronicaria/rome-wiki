France's 2027 presidential election is scheduled for April 18 and May 2, but its outcome will be filtered through ballot-access, campaign-finance, two-round, and parliamentary institutions that polling alone cannot capture.

Up: [[Politics]] · Related: [[Political prediction markets]]

## Current state

The Interior Ministry lists the first round for April 18, 2027 and the runoff for May 2. Candidate labels must distinguish discussed, exploring, declared, party-selected, and formally qualified people.

The Commission des sondages expanded systematic scrutiny in April 2026 to surveys of political popularity and notoriety because they increasingly shape the pre-campaign. Its notice archive is the canonical source for field dates, sample, sponsor, tested slate, and adjustments.

The archive added four presidential-tagged notices dated July 8: two political-image/barometer studies and two studies framed around Marine Le Pen's appeal conviction and possible candidacy. These notices establish what was tested, not that any candidacy status changed. Scenario comparison still requires extracting field dates, sample, mode, sponsor, wording, and tested slate.
