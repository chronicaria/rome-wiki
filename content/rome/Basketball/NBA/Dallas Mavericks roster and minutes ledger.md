## The controlling roster record

The first job of a roster ledger is identity, not projection. Dallas officially announced on July 8 that it acquired Santi Aldama, Marcus Sasser, and the NBA rights to Tarik Biberovic in a six-team transaction. The Mavericks sent out Khris Middleton and AJ Johnson, as well as draft compensation. The NBA's offseason trade tracker independently records the completed transaction. Those dated event records control even though the Mavericks' public roster grid still displayed Middleton and Johnson and omitted Aldama and Sasser when checked July 10.

That conflict is a publication lag, not evidence that the trade was reversed. A roster page is a useful display, but a dated transaction announcement is the stronger source for whether a transaction occurred. The NBA player pages already identify Aldama and Sasser with Dallas, adding a second official cross-check. Therefore this ledger assigns no Dallas role or minutes to Middleton or Johnson and includes Aldama and Sasser in the basketball inventory.

One correction is especially important because stale depth charts can propagate quickly: **Tyus Jones is not a current Maverick.** Dallas's official 2025-26 game notes record that it waived Jones on February 28 after an eight-game Dallas stint. Denver then announced his signing on March 5, and official Nuggets game notes list him on Denver's roster. Assigning Jones Dallas minutes would be a factual roster error, not a debatable forecast choice.

The ledger also separates NBA employment from rights ownership. Dallas acquiring Biberovic's NBA rights does not establish an NBA contract, a roster place, or availability for training camp. The same caution applies to other draft-rights players. A summer-league appearance, draft selection, camp invitation, two-way deal, Exhibit 10 agreement, standard contract, and guaranteed opening-night roster place are different states. Names are not interchangeable with roster slots.

## Evidence labels used here

**Official** means a fact established by a team or league transaction record, contract announcement, roster record, or medical statement. **Reported** means credible journalism that has not been matched here to an official filing. **Inference** means a basketball conclusion drawn from verified personnel, roles, or past performance. **Forecast** means a proposed 2026-27 minute range or rotation branch. **Unknown** means the reviewed public evidence does not resolve the question.

This distinction matters because a clean-looking depth chart can hide several different claims. “Kyrie Irving is under contract,” “Irving is expected back,” “Irving will be active on opening night,” and “Irving will play 32 minutes at his prior level” are not one proposition. Only the first two are supported in the reviewed official record. The participation date, workload, back-to-back plan, and functional performance remain unknown.

## The 240-minute accounting problem

Every NBA game contains 240 player-minutes before overtime: five players multiplied by 48 regulation minutes. A useful preseason ledger allocates those minutes by role and scenario rather than pretending to predict exact season averages. Ranges should answer three questions: who has a credible rotation path, which absences force structural changes, and where competition remains open?

The Dallas problem has two unusually large switches. Irving determines whether Dallas has its only proven elite half-court guard creator. Lively determines whether Dallas can build its center rotation around a mobile rim protector and vertical spacer or must lean more heavily on Gafford and offense-first frontcourt alternatives. Their statuses interact. When both are active, Dallas can pair a premier pick-and-roll creator with vertical centers and ask Flagg to attack a tilted defense. When Irving is limited, Flagg, Nembhard, Sasser, Marshall, and other connectors must create more possessions. When Lively is limited, [[Dallas center rotation without full Lively availability|the center solution]] changes the defensive and spacing responsibilities around those creators.

This is why one fixed “minutes projection” would be false precision. The following branches are forecasts, not reports of coaching decisions.

## Forecast branch A — Irving and Lively functionally available

This branch assumes both players can participate without a severe game-to-game restriction. It does **not** assume they immediately reproduce career-best performance.

| Role cluster | Forecast regulation minutes | Allocation logic |
|---|---:|---|
| Irving | 28–32 | Primary creation with deliberate workload protection after a long absence |
| Flagg | 32–35 | Two-way centerpiece and co-creator; avoids making every possession his initiation test |
| Washington, Marshall, Christie | 70–82 combined | Main two-way forward/wing bridge across starting and bench groups |
| Thompson, Martin | 22–36 combined | Shooting and veteran wing minutes, adjusted for matchup and defensive fit |
| Lively, Gafford | 38–44 combined | Most center minutes, with neither forced into an extreme workload |
| Aldama | 22–28 | Skilled forward beside a center plus selective offense-first minutes at five |
| Nembhard, Sasser and reserve guards | 20–32 combined | Organization, second-side shooting, and non-Irving ballhandling |
| Remaining depth | 0–12 | Matchup, development, injury substitution, or earned camp competition |

These ranges overlap because players span clusters and because the table describes a feasible envelope, not an instruction to add every maximum. A specific nightly plan must total 240. A plausible healthy-shape example could devote roughly 31 minutes to Irving, 34 to Flagg, 29 to Washington, 27 to Marshall, 25 to Christie, 24 to Aldama, 22 to Lively, 20 to Gafford, and the remaining 28 across Thompson, Nembhard, Sasser, Martin, and matchup depth. That illustration shows the squeeze: Dallas has more credible veterans and prospects than it has stable nightly minutes.

The healthy branch creates competition rather than eliminating uncertainty. Aldama can absorb minutes at both frontcourt positions, but playing him at center trades some rim protection and physical rebounding for shooting and passing. Thompson remains a unique movement-shooting threat, but he should not automatically receive closing minutes if a defensive matchup demands Christie, Marshall, or Washington. Nembhard may be the cleanest organizer behind Irving, while Sasser may supply more pull-up scoring. Their functions can coexist; neither should be labeled a complete Irving substitute.

## Forecast branch B — Irving active but managed

“Active” may include a minute ceiling, no back-to-backs, shorter shifts, or diminished burst. None of those policies has been officially specified. If Irving plays roughly 20–28 minutes rather than a normal lead-guard load, Dallas must redistribute eight to twelve creation minutes without expecting one reserve to inherit his whole job.

Flagg's on-ball work would rise first. Nembhard could organize more half-court entries and protect possession quality. Sasser could receive additional bench scoring possessions, especially beside a larger initiator. Marshall and Aldama can advance the ball, attack a closeout, and connect actions. Thompson's gravity can simplify decisions for all of them. The mechanism is **distributed creation**: several players replace pieces of Irving's workload, while no claim is made that they replace his handle, shot-making, rim pressure, passing manipulation, and late-clock rescue as a package.

A managed-Irving plan should also stagger him from Flagg enough to stabilize bench units without depriving Flagg of reps as a primary initiator. The coaching tradeoff is development versus possession quality. Too much separation asks Irving to carry fragile lineups and increases workload. Too little separation leaves non-Irving minutes without a tested organizer and delays learning about Flagg's ceiling. [[Dallas guard creation after roster turnover]] treats this as a portfolio problem rather than a depth-chart label.

## Forecast branch C — Lively delayed or restricted

If Lively cannot handle a normal center load, Gafford becomes the foundation rather than one half of a balanced tandem. A reasonable scenario envelope gives Gafford roughly 24–30 minutes, Aldama 6–14 situational minutes at center, and a verified depth center or small-ball group the remainder. This is not a claim that Aldama replaces Lively defensively. It is a way to cover the clock while changing the scheme.

Dallas would need to redistribute Lively's functions across the lineup. Gafford supplies vertical finishing, shot blocking, screening, and physicality, but his offense is also creator-dependent. Aldama supplies shooting, passing, and five-out possibility, but lineups around him must rebound and protect the rim collectively. Washington, Flagg, Marshall, and Christie would carry more help-defense and rebounding responsibility. The team could also use matchup-specific depth, but no unverified player should receive forecast minutes simply because a roster slot appears open.

The Lively branch interacts with Irving's status. Without both players, Dallas loses its cleanest creator-center partnership and cannot solve the offense by merely increasing Gafford's minutes. The ballhandler must first force the defense to react before a rim-running center can exploit the advantage. That makes Flagg development, Nembhard organization, Sasser pull-up shooting, and Aldama connectivity more important at the same time that the defense becomes harder to stabilize.

## Forecast branch D — both high-leverage players unavailable

This is the stress case, not the baseline prediction. It should remain in the ledger because projections that omit correlated downside overstate certainty. Flagg would become the central initiator and likely the highest-minute player. Gafford would anchor conventional center minutes. Washington, Marshall, Christie, and Aldama would form the principal size-and-skill bridge. Nembhard and Sasser would compete for larger guard responsibilities, while Thompson's shooting would become even more valuable to preserve space.

The stress case changes goals as well as minutes. Dallas might prioritize development and lineup discovery over squeezing every veteran minute for marginal short-term gain. That is an inference from roster age and uncertainty, not an announced organizational policy. The responsible forecast should present a performance range wide enough to reflect that choice rather than quietly assuming perfect health and win-now deployment.

## Rotation competitions that remain real

**The second organizer.** Nembhard has the clearest verified organizational path, but organization is not the same as scoring pressure. Sasser's shooting and pull-up ability may fit better beside Flagg in some lineups. Dallas can use both if roles are complementary and defense holds; the competition is for specific tasks, not merely a “backup point guard” title.

**The wing squeeze.** Marshall, Christie, Washington, Thompson, Martin, and Flagg can consume almost all non-center forward and wing minutes before Aldama is counted. The eventual allocation will reveal Dallas's priorities: movement shooting, point-of-attack defense, size, secondary handling, or development. Reputation should not settle the contest in advance.

**The Aldama position split.** [[Santi Aldama in Dallas lineups]] argues that his cleanest base role is skilled forward beside a true center. His center minutes are still strategically important because they open the floor. The split will depend on Lively's health, Gafford's load, opponent size, and whether Dallas can defend and rebound around a lighter five.

**The final depth places.** Official display names and development players must pass a contract-status gate before they pass a basketball gate. After that, shooting, positional size, defensive reliability, and the ability to survive low-usage minutes should determine who has a realistic role. The ledger should not reserve minutes for an unresolved contract.

## Update protocol

This note should change when an official transaction, signing, waiver, conversion, option decision, or medical milestone changes a branch. Each update should record the event date and research date separately. A roster-grid correction can resolve the display conflict, but it should not erase the transaction chronology that explains why the earlier grid was stale.

Minutes should move only after the relevant fact changes. A standard signing moves a player from rights or camp status into the NBA inventory; it does not guarantee rotation minutes. Medical clearance moves a player toward participation; it does not prove unrestricted workload or restored effectiveness. A preseason role quote is evidence of intent; it is not a binding regular-season allocation. Actual games will eventually replace forecast ranges with observed role distributions, ideally over enough possessions to avoid treating one matchup or injury substitution as a permanent hierarchy.

## Why it matters

Roster mistakes are upstream errors. If Tyus Jones is incorrectly left on Dallas, if Middleton or Johnson remain because a stale page was scraped, or if Biberovic's rights are treated as a signed contract, every downstream lineup, cap, and team projection begins from the wrong population. Even a sophisticated player model cannot repair invalid roster inputs.

Minute mistakes are equally consequential. Player value becomes team value through availability and role. A projection that gives Irving 34 unrestricted minutes and Lively a normal season without branching has embedded two optimistic medical assumptions while presenting them as arithmetic. A ledger with conditional ranges makes those assumptions visible. It connects [[NBA roster churn as projection uncertainty]] to the specific Dallas questions in [[Dallas Mavericks model pressure points]] and gives future analysis a reproducible starting point.

The practical conclusion is restrained but useful: Dallas has a deep group of credible forwards and centers, a singular high-end creator whose return remains the largest swing, and several narrower guard options rather than a verified substitute for him. The team can construct a rotation today. It cannot yet justify one exact rotation.
