## Current state as of 2026-07-10

This article is a public-safe translation layer, not a model release. It does not disclose private files, formulas, player coefficients, simulation weights, or a new win total. It asks a narrower question: which verified public developments should force a forecaster to reconsider minutes, roles, player factors, lineup interactions, or the width of the team-outcome distribution?

Four facts define the current stress test. First, Dallas officially acquired Santi Aldama and Marcus Sasser, along with Tarik Biberovic's NBA rights, on July 8 while sending out Khris Middleton and AJ Johnson plus draft compensation. Second, the official Dallas roster grid still lagged that transaction when checked July 10, so the transaction announcement—not the stale grid—is the controlling event source. Third, Dallas ruled Kyrie Irving out for all of 2025-26 while he continued recovering from left-knee ACL reconstruction; later official-facing material expressed an expectation that he could be ready for 2026-27, but no reviewed source establishes unrestricted participation. Fourth, Dereck Lively II's 2025-26 season ended after seven games and right-foot surgery, with the last public milestone reviewed here not establishing a normal offseason workload.

The roster has therefore improved in some visible ways without becoming easy to forecast. Aldama adds size, shooting, and connective passing. Sasser adds another guard who can handle and score. Cooper Flagg enters year two after winning Rookie of the Year. Dusty May has articulated a faster, passing-oriented identity. Yet Irving remains the only rostered player with a long NBA record as an elite primary half-court creator, while Lively remains the center with the clearest combination of rim protection, mobility, vertical spacing, and connective upside. The most important assumptions are correlated: one player's absence changes the role and efficiency environment of several others.

## Evidence labels and forecast language

This note uses four labels deliberately.

**Fact** means a dated public source establishes the transaction, performance record, health announcement, or organizational statement. A fact can still be incomplete. “Acquired” establishes team control; it does not establish an October rotation. “Expected to be ready” records an expectation; it does not establish clearance.

**Inference** means a basketball consequence follows plausibly from the fact but has not yet been observed in 2026-27 competition. For example, more Irving absence should increase Flagg's initiation burden. That is a role inference, not proof that Flagg's efficiency will rise or fall by a particular amount.

**Unknown** means the public record does not justify a settled input. Exact games played, minute restrictions, back-to-back policy, opening-night lineups, and the effectiveness of May's system are unknown.

**Model pressure** means the evidence should prompt a sensitivity test or a changed assumption class. It does not mean the public fact can be converted honestly into a precise number of wins. A claimed “plus 2.3 wins” without a documented rerun, uncertainty interval, and interaction treatment would manufacture precision.

The correct output is therefore a pressure map: what moves, in which direction, through what mechanism, and what future evidence could confirm or reverse the move.

## Pressure point one: Irving is three variables, not one

**Fact:** Dallas announced in February that Irving would miss the remainder of 2025-26 while continuing ACL rehabilitation. NBA and Mavericks material later described him as expected to return for 2026-27 and as a foundation for May's team. The reviewed evidence does not establish full-contact clearance, unrestricted five-on-five work, an opening-night minute target, or a back-to-back policy.

**Inference:** Irving changes Dallas even before he approaches his old box-score production because he supplies scarce possession functions. He can bring the ball up under pressure, create the first defensive rotation, make a pull-up punish a defender who goes under, and produce a credible late-clock attempt when the designed action fails. Those functions improve the shots available to Lively, Daniel Gafford, Aldama, shooters, and cutters. They also allow Flagg to attack more tilted floors rather than beginning every difficult possession against five set defenders.

**Model pressure:** separate Irving into participation, workload, and effectiveness variables. Games active should not mechanically restore old per-minute value. A controlled 22-minute role could have considerable lineup value if concentrated around creation-needy units and closing possessions; 30 nominal minutes could disappoint if change-of-direction, rim pressure, or defensive movement remains impaired. The model should also avoid treating Irving's minutes as independent of teammates' rates. His presence can lower Flagg's usage while improving Flagg's efficiency, increase a rolling center's finishing opportunities, and make an offense-first spacer easier to play.

**Wins logic:** Irving creates the largest direct upside branch, but the relationship should curve rather than move linearly. The first reliable creator minutes may be more valuable than later replacement minutes because they prevent the roster from assigning primary jobs to secondary players. At the same time, a 34-year-old guard returning after an extended absence deserves a wide effectiveness band. The proper forecast response is more scenario width, not an all-or-nothing “healthy Kyrie” switch.

**Unknown:** when he will be cleared, what workload Dallas chooses, whether he can repeatedly turn the corner, and how often he can defend NBA actions without being protected. Training camp and preseason can answer participation and role questions; only repeated competitive possessions can answer effectiveness.

## Pressure point two: Lively changes the permission structure

**Fact:** Lively played only seven games in 2025-26 before season-ending right-foot surgery. Public Dallas material has described rehabilitation and hope for the coming season, but the evidence reviewed for [[Dereck Lively II health and defensive value]] does not justify assuming unrestricted training-camp work.

**Inference:** Lively's value is not confined to blocks, rebounds, or center minutes. A credible back-line defender gives perimeter teammates permission to crowd ballhandlers, fight over screens, and send drives toward help. His mobility can preserve more coverage options than a stationary drop-only center. Offensively, his screening and lob threat create a vertical constraint, while his passing can keep an advantage alive. Those jobs let Gafford remain an energetic second center and let Aldama play more often as a skilled forward rather than as an emergency defensive five.

**Model pressure:** build at least three Lively workload states: unavailable, restricted, and functionally normal. Each state needs a different minute matrix. An unavailable Lively raises Gafford's workload, increases Aldama-at-center or depth-center exposure, and transfers rebounding and rim-protection duties to Flagg, P. J. Washington, and the wings. A restricted Lively may still improve selected lineups without supporting an ordinary starter projection. A normal state raises both his minutes and the compatibility of teammates' roles.

The factor adjustment should not award his full defensive influence twice. If the player projection already captures some rim and lineup value, a separate team-defense adjustment must represent only the interaction that the underlying method omits. Conversely, a box-score-heavy prior may understate the permission structure. [[RAPM]] and lineup evidence can help interrogate the prior, but neither should be imported without sample, teammate, opponent, and role context.

**Wins logic:** Lively's return raises the ceiling most when it keeps multiple other players in appropriate roles. That makes his value correlated with Gafford, Aldama, and Flagg. The downside is also matchup-dependent: Dallas can cover nominal center minutes without him, but not every substitute lineup preserves rim deterrence, switching tolerance, rebounding, passing, and vertical gravity at once.

**Unknown:** running and contact milestones, camp repetition, sustainable stint length, and which coverages May asks him to play. A positive quote without a functional milestone should move confidence less than repeated full practices or preseason games.

## Pressure point three: Flagg's upside can be confused with overload

**Fact:** Flagg won the 2025-26 Rookie of the Year award after carrying a major role for Dallas. The official record establishes production and recognition; it does not establish the size or shape of a second-year leap.

**Inference:** Flagg is the highest-leverage healthy-roster development variable because he can affect both sides and multiple possession types. Improvement could arrive through better pull-up and catch shooting, more efficient drives, stronger passing reads, fewer live-ball turnovers, improved defensive discipline, or simply better lineup context. These are not interchangeable. A scoring increase caused by more emergency possessions is different from a shooting or decision-making gain at similar difficulty.

**Model pressure:** decompose the “year-two jump.” Minutes may remain high without changing. Offensive role should branch with Irving availability. Shooting gravity should depend not only on percentage but on volume, location, and defender response. Playmaking should distinguish creating the first advantage from making the next pass. Defensive value should account for assignment difficulty and whether Lively or another center protects the back line.

The critical correlation is burden. If Irving is functional, Flagg can grow as a co-creator, screener, transition initiator, and second-side attacker. If Irving is limited, Flagg may accumulate more on-ball events while facing loaded defenses and late clocks. A naive model may read higher usage as development or apply the same efficiency curve in both contexts. The pressure test should instead ask whether his role is becoming more valuable or merely larger.

**Wins logic:** a real creation-and-shooting leap would lift Dallas's ceiling because it reduces dependence on one veteran guard and permits more large defensive lineups. But overload could lower efficiency, increase turnovers, and consume defensive energy. Flagg is therefore both an upside driver and the main conduit through which other injuries become nonlinear.

**Unknown:** the balance May chooses among initiation, screening, transition, and defensive assignments; whether defenders honor the jumper; and whether improved outcomes persist after controlling for Irving and lineup context.

## Pressure point four: Aldama adds options, not automatic value

**Fact:** Dallas officially acquired Aldama on July 8. NBA statistics show that he brought credible three-point volume, passing production, and frontcourt size in 2025-26. His official player-news chronology also records a season-ending right-knee procedure in March, so unrestricted fall availability is not established by the trade.

**Inference:** Aldama's cleanest base role is skilled forward beside Lively or Gafford. His shooting can pull a frontcourt defender from the lane; his passing can extend an advantage; his size can preserve rebounding and finishing. At center he can create five-out spacing and force slow bigs to defend away from the rim, but Dallas then transfers rim protection, physical box-outs, and some containment work to the rest of the lineup.

**Model pressure:** do not enter Aldama as a uniform talent upgrade. Assign separate forward and center shares, each conditioned on his health and Lively's availability. His offensive factor may rise in lineups with an advantage creator and a rolling center, while his defensive factor can vary sharply by assignment. Center minutes may improve spacing but worsen rim defense or rebounding. Forward minutes may preserve role fit but compress minutes for Washington, Naji Marshall, or other large wings.

**Wins logic:** Aldama expands the number of viable lineup solutions, which can raise the matchup ceiling and reduce the cost of some injuries. Optionality has value only if the coach can identify combinations that cover all five jobs. It is not equal to adding each player's isolated estimate. [[Lineup fit is not the sum of player value]] is the governing principle.

**Unknown:** clearance after the knee procedure, his base defensive assignment, how frequently May uses him at center, and whether opponents guard his shot tightly enough to create the intended space.

## Pressure point five: secondary guards determine the floor

**Fact:** Dallas acquired Marcus Sasser and retained other handling options including Ryan Nembhard and secondary initiators on the wing. Tyus Jones is not a current Maverick. No public source establishes that any non-Irving guard can reproduce Irving's complete creation role.

**Inference:** Sasser can add pull-up willingness, scoring, and ballhandling; Nembhard can help organize possessions; Christie, Marshall, Aldama, and Flagg can extend advantages or initiate selected actions. Collectively, that group can cover ball-touch volume. Covering touches is not the same as creating efficient shots against a set defense. The distinction matters most when Irving sits and opponents load toward Flagg.

**Model pressure:** transferred minutes should carry transferred difficulty. Promoting a reserve scorer into primary creation should not preserve his lower-usage efficiency automatically. Similarly, a connector's assist rate should not be projected as though he will generate the first advantage. The rotation should distinguish organizer, advantage creator, secondary attacker, movement shooter, and defensive guard rather than collapsing all of them into “backcourt minutes.”

**Wins logic:** the secondary guards govern Dallas's floor more than its theoretical ceiling. Competent non-Irving organization can prevent disastrous bench possessions, protect Flagg from constant pressure, and activate finishers. A genuine breakout creator would create further upside, but the public evidence does not support building that into the central case.

**Unknown:** Sasser's Dallas role, Nembhard's effectiveness against starter pressure, the guard pairings May trusts, and which player closes when Irving is unavailable or managed.

## Pressure point six: coaching changes the context before it changes the rating

**Fact:** Dallas hired Dusty May in June. His official introduction emphasized pace, passing, adaptable offense, and accountability. He has not coached a Dallas regular-season game.

**Inference:** those principles fit a roster with a grab-and-go forward in Flagg, multiple passing bigs, rim-running centers, and uncertain point-guard availability. Early offense can reduce the number of possessions that begin against a set defense. Multiple ball screens and rapid reversals can let Aldama and Flagg attack a moving defense. Defensive adaptability can exploit Dallas's size.

**Model pressure:** avoid a generic “new coach bump.” Coaching initially widens uncertainty because role allocation, substitution patterns, pace, and coverages change the context of every player rate. A model can test tactical implications—more transition opportunities, different usage shares, more or fewer two-big minutes—without assigning May an unsupported standalone win value.

**Wins logic:** scheme can reduce the frequency of Dallas's hardest problems, but it cannot erase missing skills. Pace does not replace late-clock creation. Switching does not create a rim protector. Passing does not help if no player bends the first defender. May's influence should emerge through observed role and lineup evidence, not reputation.

**Unknown:** actual NBA pace, who initiates, which frontcourt combinations close, how often Dallas switches, and whether the intended principles survive opponents' scouting.

## The scenario grid that should replace a single depth chart

The public record supports a compact scenario grid, not probability claims:

1. **Irving and Lively functionally available.** Dallas can keep Flagg in a co-creation role, share center minutes, and use Aldama primarily as a skilled forward. This is the cleanest upside structure, but it still requires evidence of effectiveness and continuity.
2. **Irving available, Lively limited.** Creation remains organized; Gafford absorbs traditional-center work and Aldama supplies selective five-out minutes. Defensive and rebounding matchup variance rises.
3. **Lively available, Irving limited.** Dallas retains a defensive skeleton but asks Flagg and the secondary guards to manufacture offense. Shooting around the creators becomes more important.
4. **Both limited.** Role cascades compound: Flagg carries more creation and defense, Gafford's workload rises, Aldama fills more structural gaps, and reserve guards face harder possessions. This is the nonlinear downside case.
5. **Both active but below old effectiveness.** The most easily missed branch. Binary availability looks positive while burst, workload, coverage range, or continuity remains below the prior assumption.

Each branch should have its own minute matrix and role definitions. Only after reliable evidence exists should a forecast assign weights. Even then, the outputs should show a range and describe what drives it. The previous Dallas briefing can remain a dated baseline; this page records why the next rerun may move and why a judgmental adjustment must never be presented as a completed rerun.

## Verification triggers

The pressure map should update when evidence changes, not when optimism or anxiety becomes louder.

For Irving, meaningful positive triggers include full-contact practice, repeated preseason participation, demonstrated rim pressure and change-of-direction, and ordinary rather than exceptional workload management. For Lively, they include running, basketball activity, full contact, repeated practice, and sustained movement through defensive coverages. For Aldama, they include clearance after the knee procedure and evidence of the defensive role Dallas intends.

For Flagg and the guards, the best evidence is role-based: who initiates against a set defense, who draws two defenders, what happens after the first action, and how efficiency changes by lineup. For May, track possession style and lineup choices rather than press-conference vocabulary. Once games begin, official NBA injury reports establish availability designations; film and statistics explain role and performance but should state the sample and context.

Negative triggers include delayed clearance, repeated non-contact status, a stated restriction extending into the season, role promotions that reduce efficiency, and lineup families that cannot defend the rim or create a first advantage. A single practice clip, made jumper, block, or preseason plus-minus is weak evidence.

## Why this framing matters

Dallas's roster is deep in bodies and broad in possible lineups, but it is not redundant in its most important functions. Irving owns the clearest elite-creator history. Lively offers the most complete two-way center concept. Flagg connects the present roster to the next competitive window. Aldama and Sasser add real optionality while bringing new health, role, and fit questions. May can reorganize the environment, but his system is still a hypothesis.

A useful forecast respects that structure. It separates minutes from role, participation from effectiveness, and individual value from lineup interaction. It recognizes that the same Flagg usage can mean healthy development in one branch and emergency overload in another. It lets a Lively return affect not only his own projection but also Gafford's workload, Aldama's position, and the perimeter's defensive behavior. It credits Irving's ability to improve other players' shot environments without pretending any return recreates his prior self automatically.

The result is less theatrical than a precise win-delta headline and more useful. The public can see which assumptions matter, which direction each would push, and which observations would falsify the story. The private model can remain private. When the next verified roster, health, or role fact arrives, Dallas's forecast should change through an explicit causal path rather than through an unexplained manual number.
