Dereck Lively II can raise Dallas's defensive ceiling through rim deterrence, spatial coverage, communication, and lineup flexibility, but a right-foot surgery and a June recovery stage short of running make his 2026-27 workload an unresolved variable rather than a healthy-roster assumption.

Up: [[Dallas Mavericks 2026-27 season]] · Team context: [[Dallas Mavericks availability scenarios]] · Frontcourt context: [[Dallas frontcourt geometry]]

## The question is availability multiplied by function

Lively is not merely one more center in a deep frontcourt. At his best, he changes where opponents can attack and how aggressively Dallas's other defenders can behave. His length and vertical reach protect the basket; his mobility lets him participate above the restricted area; his communication can organize the back line; and his ability to recover after helping preserves more of the possession than a stationary shot blocker would. Those mechanisms make his health unusually important even when Daniel Gafford can competently absorb center minutes.

The correct question is therefore not simply whether Lively will play. It is whether he can progress through running, basketball activity, contact, repeated practices, games, and sustained NBA loading while retaining the movement qualities that create his defensive value. Public evidence as of July 10 does not answer that question. It establishes a serious recent foot history and a dated rehabilitation milestone. It does not establish a return date, minutes limit, back-to-back policy, recurrence probability, or post-return effectiveness.

This article uses three labels to maintain that boundary:

- **Official:** a Mavericks, NBA, or league document establishes the event or status.
- **Reported:** an attributable journalist report supplies a milestone not stated in an official release.
- **Inference:** a basketball conclusion follows conditionally from the known role or roster, not from private medical knowledge.

“Unknown” is not a negative diagnosis. It means the public record does not support a more precise claim.

## What is verified about the foot and recovery

The recent history is cumulative and should be read chronologically.

**Official, January 2025:** Dallas announced that Lively had a right-ankle stress fracture and would be re-evaluated in four weeks. A later Mavericks update said he had missed 24 games, was progressing in rehabilitation, and was doing individual court work. The 2024-25 season ended with 36 appearances, following 55 as a rookie. Games played alone cannot identify future medical risk, but it does establish that his second NBA season did not provide a normal full-season workload base.

**Official team reporting, 2025 offseason and fall:** Mavericks coverage said Lively underwent a procedure on his right foot to clean up bone spurs. Early in the 2025-26 season, a right-knee injury—first listed as a contusion and later described as a sprain—cost him nine games. When he returned on November 14, he played 17 minutes under a restriction. Team coverage explicitly connected the restriction to the summer foot procedure and knee recovery. This matters because his seven appearances were not seven unrestricted tests of a healthy baseline.

**Official, December 2025:** Dallas announced that Lively required season-ending surgery on his right foot after persistent pain, soreness, swelling, and discomfort. NBA and team accounts place the operation in London. He finished 2025-26 with seven games at 16.5 minutes per game. The official NBA player statistics show 4.3 points, 5.3 rebounds, 1.9 assists, 1.4 steals, and 0.6 blocks per game, but the sample is too small and too restricted to function as a clean estimate of his underlying level.

**Official team reporting, April 15, 2026:** Mavericks coverage said Lively had spent months on crutches and had recently advanced to a walking boot. The same article described readiness for September training camp as a hope, not a clearance or guaranteed timetable. That distinction is essential: organizational hope is relevant evidence about the intended plan, but it is not proof that the next rehabilitation milestones have occurred.

**Reported, June 26, 2026:** Christian Clark reported that Lively was out of the boot and walking, swimming, and lifting weights, but had not begun running. Lively was quoted as saying he was taking extra time so that he would not have to deal with the foot again. This is the latest specific functional milestone located for this review. It is more informative than a generic “progressing” description, but it remains a snapshot from June 26. It does not imply that he still was not running on July 10, and it does not establish when he will be cleared.

**Unknown as of July 10:** the precise operative diagnosis and procedure details beyond the team's public description; imaging results; healing criteria; current running or on-court status after June 26; contact clearance; expected training-camp participation; regular-season return date; planned minute or schedule restrictions; and individualized recurrence risk.

These unknowns prohibit a medically precise forecast. They do not prevent basketball planning. Dallas can model conditional states and update them when public milestones arrive.

## The defensive mechanisms Lively supplies

### Rim deterrence begins before a shot

Blocks record only attempts that occur and are touched. A rim protector's larger contribution can be altering the attacker's decision before release: a guard pulls up, dribbles through the lane, makes an emergency pass, or takes a higher, more difficult angle because the center occupies the basket area. Lively's length gives him a large contest radius. His mobility helps him arrive without permanently conceding the lob or offensive rebound.

That distinction explains why his value should not be reduced to blocks per game. In 2023-24 he averaged 1.4 blocks in 23.5 minutes, and in 2024-25 he averaged 1.6 in 23.1 minutes. Those are useful descriptive facts, not complete measurements of deterrence. Opponent shooting at the rim, rim-attempt frequency, foul rates, scheme, teammate quality, and possession context all matter. A center can record a block after the defense has already broken down; he can also erase an action without receiving a box-score event.

For Dallas, the strategic benefit is permission. If Lively is positioned behind the play, a perimeter defender can crowd a ball handler, fight over a screen, or funnel a drive toward help with greater confidence. Cooper Flagg can take an aggressive assignment knowing that a beaten first line need not become an uncontested layup. P. J. Washington, Naji Marshall, and Max Christie can use their size higher on the floor. This is an inference from lineup function, not evidence that every Lively lineup will defend well.

### Pick-and-roll defense is a sequence, not a coverage label

Calling Lively a “drop center” would describe only one starting position. In a conservative drop, he must contain the ball handler long enough for the guard to recover while protecting the roll and rim. That requires depth judgment: too low concedes a pull-up or runway; too high exposes the lob and pocket pass. His length can compress those choices because he can contest from farther away.

Dallas can also ask him to show higher, hedge briefly, switch late in a clock, or recover from the level of the screen. The value lies less in claiming universal switchability than in preserving multiple answers. A center who can move to the ball and still retreat toward the basket prevents the offense from knowing exactly where the weak point will be. Lively's 2024 playoff run provided high-leverage evidence that he could survive changing actions and make back-line reads, but one postseason does not guarantee the same movement after foot surgery.

The foot matters here at a basketball level without requiring a medical diagnosis. Screen coverage demands repeated acceleration, deceleration, lateral movement, turning, jumping, and recovery. Running clearance is therefore an important prerequisite, but it is not the final proof. The relevant later evidence will be repeated possession quality: can he establish the right angle, change direction, contest vertically, recover to the roller, and do it again after a short rest?

### Verticality is valuable only when it avoids the foul

Lively can contest above the rim without needing to swipe down. When he arrives balanced, his size allows him to present a vertical obstacle and keep the possession alive for a rebound. When he arrives late or chases every block, the same play can become a foul, an offensive rebound, or a pass to the abandoned center.

Foul control therefore connects directly to availability and role. He averaged 2.6 fouls in 23.5 minutes as a rookie, 2.8 in 23.1 minutes in 2024-25, and 3.0 in only 16.5 minutes across the restricted seven-game 2025-26 sample. The last number should not be extrapolated because the sample is tiny. It does identify a practical monitoring question: can he defend starter-caliber minutes without fouls forcing the rotation away from him?

Fouls reduce more than minutes. Early foul trouble changes how aggressively a center challenges later drives and can force Dallas into an unplanned coverage or lineup. If Lively returns under a short stint pattern, two quick fouls could erase much of the intended defensive benefit. Tracking fouls by action—screen defense, late rotation, offensive rebounding, or illegal screen—will be more informative than the per-game total alone.

### The second jump completes the defense

The first contest is not a stop unless Dallas secures the ball. Lively's size and reach help on defensive rebounds, while his mobility can let him contest and return to the play. But aggressive rim protection can pull him away from box-out position. Dallas's large forwards must crack back, locate opposing bigs, and finish the possession.

This is why team rebounding around Lively should be evaluated as a five-player mechanism. A lineup may suppress shooting at the basket yet surrender enough offensive rebounds to lose the advantage. Conversely, wings may collect more rebounds precisely because Lively occupied the opposing center. Individual rebound totals alone cannot allocate credit cleanly.

### Communication organizes the other four defenders

The center often sees the entire action. He calls screens, identifies cutters, directs weak-side exchanges, and decides when to commit to the ball. Mavericks coaches and players have repeatedly praised Lively's voice and basketball intelligence in team coverage. Those descriptions are meaningful role evidence, although they are not an objective possession-level grade.

Communication is especially important for a roster learning a new system under Dusty May. [[Dallas Mavericks under Dusty May]] describes a plausible identity built around pace, passing, adaptable wings, and coverages that keep the center useful near the rim. Lively can become the organizer of that structure if he is available often enough for the group to build shared timing. Intermittent participation creates a continuity cost even if his per-minute defense remains strong: guards learn different screen calls, low men face different help windows, and closing groups cannot accumulate the same repetitions.

### Defensive value can create offense

Stops allow Dallas to play before the opposing defense is set. Lively can rebound or trigger an outlet, sprint into an early drag screen, and become a vertical target. His rim run forces a defender to retreat and can clear space for Flagg or Kyrie Irving. This is not defensive rating in a narrow box; it is the possession chain through which a defensive center changes the next offensive opportunity.

His passing adds another connection. The official 2025-26 numbers show 1.9 assists in 16.5 minutes, but seven games cannot establish a new passing level. The more durable observation is that Dallas has used him as a short-roll and interior connector. If he can catch after drawing the low defender and find the corner or cutter, opponents pay a larger price for sending two defenders to the ball. That offensive competence helps Dallas keep its best defensive center on the floor.

## Lively and Gafford are complements, not identical replacements

Daniel Gafford gives Dallas a legitimate traditional center when Lively is absent. He screens, finishes forcefully, rebounds, and protects the rim. That depth lowers the danger of allocating all center minutes to an unproven player or a small lineup. It does not make Lively's health irrelevant.

**Inference:** Lively offers the higher ceiling for spatial coverage and connective play. His length and movement can permit Dallas to vary the center's screen position, and his passing can keep an advantage alive. Gafford offers a more established source of force, finishing, and present availability. The exact size of their defensive difference should be tested, not assumed from archetype.

The pairing also protects both players when both are available. Dallas can divide the 48 center minutes, keep stints energetic, respond to foul trouble, and choose a closing center by opponent and game state. Neither player must carry an extreme workload. A Lively delay pushes Gafford from one half of a center rotation toward its foundation and may require Santi Aldama, a young center, or a smaller frontcourt to cover the remainder.

Those alternatives change the defense. Aldama adds passing and spacing but should not be labeled a substitute for Lively's rim protection. A Flagg–Washington–Aldama frontcourt can switch and attack offensively, yet it may need earlier nail help and collective rebounding. A young center may reproduce size while missing positioning reads. The replacement question is therefore functional: which combinations preserve rim deterrence, rebounding, screen coverage, and communication at the same time?

## A return-to-play ladder for basketball evaluation

The public outlook should update gradually rather than flip from “injured” to “healthy.” The following ladder is observational, not medical advice:

1. **Running:** necessary evidence that rehabilitation has advanced beyond the June 26 snapshot. Straight-line running alone says little about NBA defensive movement.
2. **Individual basketball activity:** shooting, screening footwork, jumping, and controlled change of direction show task progression but lack contact and reaction.
3. **Contact and team practice:** participation against moving bodies tests timing and decision-making. One practice is weaker evidence than repeated full sessions.
4. **Preseason participation:** game movement supplies visible evidence about coverage depth, recovery, jumping, and stint length. Preseason opponent quality and effort limit statistical conclusions.
5. **Regular-season availability:** official active status confirms participation, not unrestricted workload or restored effectiveness.
6. **Repeated loading:** multiple games, normal recovery between games, and later back-to-back decisions reveal whether Dallas can build the rotation around him.
7. **Role restoration:** sustained center responsibilities, closing consideration, and movement quality support—not prove—a return toward prior basketball value.

An official minutes limit would be important but not necessarily negative. A controlled ramp can still give Dallas high-value minutes and maintain Gafford's role. The forecast should separate three variables: probability of playing, expected minutes conditional on playing, and effectiveness within those minutes. [[Availability risk in NBA projections]] explains why merging them into one games-played number produces false precision.

## What to measure after he returns

Early box scores will tempt overreaction. One multi-block game cannot establish restored defensive mobility; one scoreless game cannot establish failure. Dallas should be evaluated through repeated mechanisms:

- screen-coverage position and the types of coverage he is asked to play;
- rim attempts conceded while he is the primary back-line defender, with opponent and lineup context;
- contest quality and foul rate rather than blocks alone;
- recovery from ball pressure to the roller or rebound;
- defensive-rebound completion after he helps;
- stint length, rest pattern, and back-to-back participation;
- who closes games at center and why;
- lineup performance with comparable teammates, avoiding raw on/off attribution;
- offensive screening, rim running, and short-roll decisions that keep him playable in high-leverage minutes.

The comparison baseline should include his own 2023-24 and 2024-25 roles, Gafford lineups, and Dallas's smaller alternatives. Raw defensive rating is heavily affected by opponent shooting, teammate combinations, and schedule. Rim-defense percentages are noisy in small samples and depend on who is credited as the defender. Film and tracking should test the same mechanism rather than compete as isolated verdicts.

## Health-conditioned rotation states

### Full functional availability

If Lively completes camp, participates without a material public restriction, and handles repeated games, Dallas can organize a two-center rotation with Gafford and select between them. Lively's mobility would support more aggressive perimeter pressure and give Flagg freedom to take harder assignments. This is the high defensive-ceiling branch, not the verified current state.

### Available with a managed workload

If he is active for shorter stints or selected games, Dallas can target his minutes. He could start to establish the defensive tone, anchor a bench group, or appear in matchups that demand more coverage range. Gafford would carry the stable base. The main risk is continuity: changing restrictions can prevent lineups from learning one defensive rhythm.

### Delayed return

If he does not reach full basketball work by camp or is held out to begin the season, Gafford becomes the primary center and the remaining minutes become a strategic choice. Aldama-at-five lineups trade rim protection for skill. Young depth trades experience for development. Dallas may need its wings to protect the nail earlier and rebound more collectively. A delay should change team projections through these replacement lineups, not through a generic injury deduction.

### Active but below prior movement level

This middle case is easy to miss. Lively could be cleared and still show less range, shorter bursts, or conservative coverages while rebuilding conditioning. These are hypothetical basketball states, not claims about his likely recovery. Analysts should not restore his prior defensive value simply because he appears in the starting lineup.

## Why it matters

Dallas's 2026-27 upside depends on converting a large, versatile roster into coherent lineups. Lively is the frontcourt piece most capable of making defense easier for everyone else without sacrificing vertical offense. A healthy and effective version lets Dallas pressure the ball, vary screen coverages, protect the rim, finish possessions, and keep size on the floor. It also allows Gafford to remain a high-energy complement rather than the only proven traditional center.

The uncertainty is equally structural. As of July 10, the latest located functional report was that Lively was walking, swimming, and lifting but had not begun running on June 26. That fact should prevent a casual healthy-roster assumption. It should not be converted into a predicted return month or a pessimistic career judgment. The disciplined position is conditional: define the functions, identify the replacement costs, and update the outlook when the next observable milestone arrives.
