## Update protocol

Add a dated line only when the source changes a meaningful state: surgery or confirmed injury, a stated timetable, running, individual basketball work, contact work, full practice, game availability, a formal restriction, recurrence, or removal from the injury report. Preserve old milestones so later optimism does not erase the evidence available to an earlier forecast.

For each update, record four separate items:

1. what the source actually says;
2. whether it is official, reported, inferred, or unknown;
3. what stage changed—participation, workload, or effectiveness;
4. which minutes and roles move in the Dallas scenario matrix.

Do not infer recovery from social clips, roster listings, silence, or an absence from an offseason event. Do not turn a coach's optimism into clearance. Conversely, do not preserve an old negative milestone as current after a newer official functional update supersedes it.

## Why it matters

Irving and Lively are not interchangeable injury rows. Irving controls the roster's highest-leverage creation task; Lively controls a bundle of center and defensive functions. Their joint status changes the value of teammates, not only the number of replacement minutes. The ledger exists to keep those dependencies dated and auditable while leaving medical judgments to qualified sources.
