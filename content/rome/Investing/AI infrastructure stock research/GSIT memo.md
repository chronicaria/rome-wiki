GSIT is a technically credible AI-chip moonshot with real cash runway, but it should not be promoted until Gemini-II creates named production revenue rather than validation work.

## Evidence

GSI Technology is a legacy high-performance SRAM company trying to turn Gemini-II, a compute-in-memory Associative Processing Unit, into an edge-AI hardware platform for defense, drones, smart-city surveillance, vector search, SAR, and low-power inference.

FY2026 revenue was about $25.1M, up 22% YoY, with 54.5% gross margin and a $13.2M net loss. Cash was about $67.2M at March 31, 2026, no debt, and pro forma cash may be higher after ATM completion.

The problem is explicit: company filings say APU revenue has not been material and about 99% of revenue is SRAM. Government funding, the Sentinel POC, Army SBIR work, Taiwan smart-city Phase I, and third-party benchmark work are validation, not production revenue.

## Deepening pass

Nasdaq's July 6, 2026 snapshot showed $6.82 per share, $261.6M displayed market cap, and about 1.98M average volume. The balance sheet gives GSIT time: about $67.2M cash, no debt, and roughly $70M working capital at March 31, 2026. That is the strongest survival feature in the thesis.

The technical case is real but narrow. Gemini-II looks most interesting for data-movement-bound edge workloads: vector search, RAG retrieval, SAR/image/sensor processing, GPS-denied navigation, object detection, smart-city video analytics, and low-power inference where time-to-first-token matters more than cloud-scale throughput. The Cornell/MICRO/arXiv work is the best independent technical evidence, but it does not prove broad replacement of NVIDIA Jetson/Thor, Qualcomm edge NPUs, AMD/Xilinx/Intel FPGA paths, Hailo, Ambarella, or custom defense silicon.

The investment case fails if validation never becomes procurement. FY2026 revenue was $25.1M, gross margin was 54.5%, and net loss was $13.2M, but APU revenue remained immaterial and SRAM supplied about 99% of revenue. Avnet concentration and end-customer swings still matter while R&D rises toward Plato and future APU work.

## Bear case

This may be a technically elegant architecture that never beats NVIDIA, Qualcomm, Jetson, FPGA, or custom ASIC ecosystems in actual procurement. If Gemini-II remains a demo platform, the market can reprice GSIT as a slow memory business plus cash.

## Falsifiers

- FY2027 passes with no named Gemini-II production customer or material APU revenue.
- SRAM revenue rolls over while quarterly burn stays high.
- Sentinel, Taiwan, or Army programs fail to produce follow-on orders.

## Verdict

Active research, but behind DUOT and LTRX in practical urgency. GSIT is one of the few true AI-hardware moonshots here, but promotion requires signed Gemini-II orders above $5M and then visible repeat APU revenue.
