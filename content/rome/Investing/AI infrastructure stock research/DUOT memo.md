## Evidence

DUOT is pivoting from AI rail inspection and machine vision into modular edge data centers, GPUaaS, colocation, and infrastructure procurement. Nasdaq showed $10.24 per share and roughly $301M displayed market cap on 2026-07-06, but pro forma dilution from recent issuance and pre-funded warrants likely puts economic market cap higher.

The bull case is unusually tangible for a microcap: the Hydra Host agreement is expected to generate about $176M over 36 months, with an $18M customer prepayment, 4.3 MW of deployment, and projected high gross margin. DUOT also disclosed project financing for NVIDIA B300 GPUs through a bankruptcy-remote subsidiary.

The weak point is that Q1 2026 hosting revenue was only about $30K, Q1 total revenue fell 45% YoY, and much of the 2025 growth came from unusual New APR/asset-management economics rather than mature GPU hosting.

## Deepening pass

Nasdaq's July 6, 2026 snapshot showed $10.24 per share, $301.0M displayed market cap, and about 719k average volume. That displayed market cap likely understates the true economic equity because recent common issuance, pre-funded warrants, preferred conversion, warrants, and options can put rough fully diluted shares closer to 41.3M before equity-plan reserves. At $10.24, that implies roughly $423M fully diluted market cap, or about $446M including the plan reserve.

The Hydra/GPUaaS contract is the whole thesis now. It is described as about $176M over 36 months with 80%+ projected gross margin and about $40M annual EBITDA potential, but the actual contract is not public. Public investors cannot inspect termination rights, customer acceptance milestones, payment schedule, SLA penalties, cure periods, or amendment risk. The 10-Q also says GPUaaS revenue comes from a single customer, DUOT bears full customer nonpayment risk, and Hydra does not guarantee customer credit.

The $98.1M GPU asset-backed financing improves the first-deployment survival story, but it does not remove execution risk. DUOT had already disclosed about $145M of GPU infrastructure commitments and a $35.42M Hydra deposit as of March 31, 2026. The proof gate is not the financing press release; it is clean delivery, installation, facility closing, revenue commencement, and customer payment evidence in Q2/Q3 filings.

## Bear case

The stock may be capitalizing press-release EBITDA before operations exist at scale. The unnamed end customer, related-party revenue history, preferred/warrant complexity, and repeated equity issuance make the forward contract less bankable than it first looks.

## Falsifiers

- Q2/Q3 filings do not show GPU delivery, customer acceptance, and revenue ramp.
- Another equity, preferred, or warrant financing arrives before clear cash generation.
- Customer nonpayment, contract amendment, GPU impairment, or rail-business slippage appears.

## Verdict

Active research. DUOT deserves the first full memo because the upside is directly tied to AI infrastructure, but it must be attacked through customer credit, GPU residual value, financing closure, and dilution.
