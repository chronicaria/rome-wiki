LTRX is the strongest risk-adjusted operating company in the group, but its edge-AI/drone story still needs FY2027 guidance to prove it is more than a hardware-heavy turnaround.

Moonshot upside: 6/10. Kill risk: 3.5/10.

## Evidence

Lantronix sells edge AI, industrial IoT compute, connectivity, SOMs, gateways, out-of-band management, software, and engineering services. Q3 FY2026 revenue was about $30.2M, and management raised FY2026 drone revenue expectations to about $10M-$14M.

Nine-month FY2026 revenue was down YoY, but operating cash flow was positive at about $8M, cash was about $23.5M before later offering proceeds, and debt was manageable. Post-offering liquidity improves survival odds.

The weak point is dilution and mix. The company established a $30M ATM, issued shares through it, then sold more shares in an underwritten offering. Product revenue still dominates, while software and services remain a small share.

## Deepening pass

Nasdaq's July 6, 2026 snapshot showed $5.81 per share, $223.4M displayed market cap, and about 1.34M average volume. That displayed market cap likely trails pro forma dilution after the ATM and underwritten offering; mechanical pro forma market cap is probably closer to the mid-$200M range depending on final incremental share count.

LTRX is the cleanest operating story in the top three. Q3 FY2026 revenue was $30.2M, gross margin stayed near 43%, operating cash flow was positive for the nine-month period, debt was manageable, and the June offering added roughly $32.3M net after full option exercise. The drone business is real enough to track: management raised FY2026 drone expectations to $10M-$14M and suggested FY2027 drone revenue can roughly double.

The bear case is also straightforward. Nine-month FY2026 revenue was down 4.6% YoY because a prior Gridspertise program disappeared, and Software and Services was still only 7.6% of nine-month revenue. If drones are mostly hardware modules and payload integration while customers capture the strategic value, LTRX remains an industrial IoT hardware company with AI-adjacent upside rather than a platform.

## Bear case

LTRX may be a hardware turnaround with AI vocabulary rather than a high-multiple AI platform. If drone revenue fails to scale or software/services do not expand, the equity is less moonshot and more cyclical small-cap hardware.

## Falsifiers

- FY2027 guidance fails to show double-digit growth.
- Drone revenue misses the $10M-$14M FY2026 range.
- Software/services and deferred revenue fail to expand.

## Verdict

Active research as the risk-adjusted anchor. LTRX is not the flashiest candidate, but it gives the basket one name with real revenue, improving liquidity, positive operating cash flow, and identifiable drone/edge-AI exposure.
