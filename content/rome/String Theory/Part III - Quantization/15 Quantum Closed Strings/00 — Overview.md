Quantized in light-cone gauge, a closed loop of string carries two independent oscillator towers — waves running left and right around the loop — tied together by the single constraint that survives because an unmarked loop has no place to pin $\sigma=0$. Out of the spectrum falls a massless spin-2 particle, the graviton: nobody put gravity in, and it came anyway.

## Circulating waves, not standing waves

A guitar string clamped at both ends only has standing waves. A closed loop has no clamps, so waves can *circulate* — clockwise and counterclockwise, independently. Those are the two sectors. Quantum-mechanically each sector is a copy of the harmonic-oscillator tower you already met for the open string, and the closed string is *almost* their tensor product. That "almost" carries all the physics: one leftover symmetry glues the two towers level by level, and the lowest glued excitation is a graviton.

## Gravity falls out

The open string gave a photon ([[Photon from open strings]]). The closed string gives **gravity**. At its first excited level sits a symmetric, traceless, transverse rank-2 tensor — exactly the polarization data of a linearized metric $h_{\mu\nu}$. We never wrote a dynamical metric into the action and never invoked general covariance; a graviton dropped out of quantizing a loop. Riding alongside it are two more massless fields: the antisymmetric **Kalb–Ramond** tensor $B_{\mu\nu}$ and the scalar **dilaton** $\phi$. The dilaton is the sleeper: its background value sets the string coupling, $g_s\sim e^{\langle\phi\rangle}$, so the strength of every interaction — gravity included — is a *dynamical field*, not a dialed-in constant. Closed-string theory is therefore a quantum theory of gravity in which the coupling could, in principle, be computed rather than measured.

## The logical spine (five moves)

Read the sublessons in this order; each is one link in the chain.

1. **Two towers.** The wave equation solution splits as $X^\mu=X_L^\mu(\tau+\sigma)+X_R^\mu(\tau-\sigma)$. An open string's endpoints glue these together; a closed loop has only periodicity $X^\mu(\tau,\sigma)=X^\mu(\tau,\sigma+2\pi)$, which is too weak to relate them. So $\alpha_n^\mu$ (right) and $\bar\alpha_n^\mu$ (left) survive as **two commuting oscillator algebras**. They touch in exactly one place: the shared zero mode, $\alpha_0^\mu=\bar\alpha_0^\mu=\sqrt{\alpha'/2}\,p^\mu$ — one momentum, not two. See 15.1 [[Left and right oscillator algebras]].
2. **Level matching.** You can rigidly rotate the loop, $\sigma\mapsto\sigma+\sigma_0$, without changing anything physical — there's no marked point to anchor $\sigma=0$. This unfixable symmetry is generated by the worldsheet momentum $P=L_0^\perp-\bar L_0^\perp$, and demanding invariance forces $P|\Psi\rangle=0$, i.e. **equal left and right levels** $N^\perp=\bar N^\perp$. See 15.2 [[Level matching]].
3. **Mass formula.** Solving the light-cone constraint for $M^2=-p^2$ (mostly-plus signature, $\hbar=c=1$) gives
   $$M^2=\frac{2}{\alpha'}\big(N^\perp+\bar N^\perp-2\big)\ \xrightarrow{\ N^\perp=\bar N^\perp\equiv N\ }\ \frac{4}{\alpha'}(N-1),$$
   with a normal-ordering constant $-1$ per sector (hence $-2$). See 15.3 [[Closed-string mass formula]].
4. **The massless level.** At $N^\perp=\bar N^\perp=1$ the states are $\alpha_{-1}^I\bar\alpha_{-1}^J|p^+,\vec p_T\rangle$, indexed by a $(D-2)\times(D-2)$ matrix. Split it into symmetric-traceless + antisymmetric + trace to get **graviton + Kalb–Ramond + dilaton**. See 15.4 [[Graviton antisymmetric tensor and dilaton]].
5. **The coupling.** The dilaton's expectation value fixes $g_s\sim e^{\langle\phi\rangle}$, making the coupling a field. See 15.5 [[String coupling and the dilaton]].

## The three key numbers, and where they come from

- **Ground state is a tachyon, $\alpha'M^2=-4$.** Plug $N=0$ into $M^2=\frac{4}{\alpha'}(N-1)$. This is four times more negative than the open-string tachyon ($\alpha'M^2=-1$) — a signal of vacuum instability, not a real particle.
- **Level spacing is $4/\alpha'$, not $1/\alpha'$.** Two factors of 2 multiply. The shared zero mode is half the open one ($\sqrt{\alpha'/2}\,p^\mu$ against the open $\sqrt{2\alpha'}\,p^\mu$), which puts $2/\alpha'$ in front of the levels instead of $1/\alpha'$. And level matching lets you climb only *diagonally*, $(N^\perp,\bar N^\perp)\to(N^\perp+1,\bar N^\perp+1)$, so each physical step raises $N^\perp+\bar N^\perp$ by 2. The product $2/\alpha'\times2$ is the $4/\alpha'$ spacing shared by the two forms $\frac{2}{\alpha'}(N^\perp+\bar N^\perp-2)$ and $\frac{4}{\alpha'}(N-1)$.
- **Massless at level 1 forces $D=26$.** The excited states carry only transverse $SO(D-2)$ indices, so they cannot fill out a massive $SO(D-1)$ multiplet — Lorentz invariance forces them massless. Masslessness means $\frac{4}{\alpha'}\big(1-\frac{D-2}{24}\big)=0$, i.e. $D=26$, equivalently a normal-ordering constant $a=-\frac{D-2}{24}=-1$ per sector. The full anomaly-based proof is in [[Critical dimension D=26]].

## Why "massless spin-2" *is* gravity

Not a coincidence, not merely graviton-*like*. A free symmetric tensor $h_{\mu\nu}$ carries unphysical timelike components; consistency demands the gauge redundancy $h_{\mu\nu}\to h_{\mu\nu}+\partial_\mu\xi_\nu+\partial_\nu\xi_\mu$, and any matter this field couples to must then carry a conserved source. But once $h_{\mu\nu}$ interacts it carries energy-momentum itself, so it must source itself; iterating that self-consistency (the Feynman–Weinberg bootstrap) turns the linear gauge symmetry into the full nonlinear diffeomorphism invariance of Einstein gravity. A consistent interacting massless spin-2 particle has no choice but to be a graviton. Details in 15.4 [[Graviton antisymmetric tensor and dilaton]].

## Open questions

- We take on faith here that Lorentz invariance fixes $D=26$ and $a=-1$. The zeta/cutoff computation $\sum n \to -\tfrac1{12}$ gives the right *number*, but the honest reason is that the light-cone Lorentz algebra fails to close otherwise — flagged for [[Critical dimension D=26]].
- The closed-string tachyon ($M^2=-4/\alpha'$) signals that the bosonic vacuum is a maximum, not a minimum, of some potential. Its fate is genuinely not understood; the superstring removes it.

## Exercise

Starting from the two facts you're allowed to assume — the closed-string mass formula $M^2=\frac{2}{\alpha'}(N^\perp+\bar N^\perp-2)$ and the level-matching constraint $N^\perp=\bar N^\perp$ — do all three:

(a) Write the lowest **two** allowed levels and their $M^2$, and explain why there is no physical state with $\tfrac12\alpha'M^2=-1$.
(b) Recast the formula in the single-level form $M^2=\frac{4}{\alpha'}(N-1)$ and confirm it agrees with (a).
(c) At the first excited level, the states carry a $(D-2)\times(D-2)$ index matrix. Decompose that matrix into pieces that transform independently under transverse rotations, name the field each piece becomes, and count the states for $D=26$.

> [!success]- Solution
> **(a) Lowest two allowed levels.** Level matching forces $N^\perp=\bar N^\perp$, so only diagonal levels $(N^\perp,\bar N^\perp)=(0,0),(1,1),\dots$ survive.
> - $(0,0)$: $\tfrac12\alpha'M^2=0+0-2=-2$, so $M^2=-4/\alpha'$ — a scalar **tachyon**.
> - $(1,1)$: $\tfrac12\alpha'M^2=1+1-2=0$, so $M^2=0$ — the **massless** level.
>
> A state with $\tfrac12\alpha'M^2=-1$ would need $N^\perp+\bar N^\perp=1$, i.e. $(1,0)$ or $(0,1)$. Both violate $N^\perp=\bar N^\perp$, so they are unphysical: you cannot excite one sector without the other. There is no physical closed-string state between the tachyon and the massless level.
>
> **(b) Single-level form.** Set $N^\perp=\bar N^\perp\equiv N$. Then $N^\perp+\bar N^\perp-2=2N-2=2(N-1)$, so
> $$M^2=\frac{2}{\alpha'}\cdot 2(N-1)=\frac{4}{\alpha'}(N-1).$$
> Check: $N=0\Rightarrow M^2=-4/\alpha'$ (the tachyon) and $N=1\Rightarrow M^2=0$ (massless). Agrees with (a), and the level spacing is $4/\alpha'$.
>
> **(c) Decomposing the massless matrix.** The states are $\sum_{I,J}R_{IJ}\,\alpha_{-1}^I\bar\alpha_{-1}^J|p^+,\vec p_T\rangle$ with $R_{IJ}$ an arbitrary $(D-2)\times(D-2)$ matrix. Any square matrix splits into three pieces that don't mix under rotations:
> $$R_{IJ}=\underbrace{\hat S_{IJ}}_{\text{sym., traceless}}+\underbrace{A_{IJ}}_{\text{antisym.}}+\underbrace{S'\,\delta_{IJ}}_{\text{trace}}.$$
> - $\hat S_{IJ}$ (symmetric traceless) $\to$ **graviton** $G_{\mu\nu}$: $\tfrac{(D-2)(D-1)}{2}-1=\tfrac{24\cdot25}{2}-1=\mathbf{299}$ states.
> - $A_{IJ}$ (antisymmetric) $\to$ **Kalb–Ramond field** $B_{\mu\nu}$: $\tfrac{(D-2)(D-3)}{2}=\tfrac{24\cdot23}{2}=\mathbf{276}$ states.
> - $S'\,\delta_{IJ}$ (trace) $\to$ **dilaton** $\phi$: $\mathbf{1}$ state.
>
> Total $299+276+1=576=24^2=(D-2)^2$. The count checks, and the symmetric-traceless piece — transverse, traceless, massless, rank-2 — is precisely a graviton's polarizations. It emerged from quantizing the loop; no metric was ever put in.
